## Why Segmentation Is the Single Biggest Email Lever

Brennan Dunn built two businesses that are inseparable: Double Your Freelancing (his education platform) and RightMessage (his SaaS tool for website and email personalization). They exist together because the same insight drove both:

**Most email marketing fails because it sends the same message to everyone.**

A list of 5,000 subscribers is never a monolith. It contains:
- Beginners who need foundational concepts
- Intermediates who've tried things and gotten stuck
- Advanced practitioners who are ready to buy
- Lurkers who opened once and went quiet
- Buyers who already purchased and need something adjacent

Sending the same newsletter to all five groups means every message is wrong for most people, most of the time. The beginner gets overwhelmed. The advanced practitioner feels patronized. The buyer gets pitched something they already own.

Segmentation solves this. When you know which group someone belongs to, you can send them the message that's actually relevant to where they are. This is why segmented emails consistently outperform broadcast emails on open rate, click rate, conversion, and unsubscribe avoidance.

## The Segmentation Framework: Three Data Types

Dunn's approach to segmentation works with three types of subscriber data:

### 1. Explicit Data (What They Tell You)
Information subscribers provide directly — through a quiz, a survey, a sign-up form, or a tag-triggering click.

Examples:
- "What's your biggest challenge right now?" (quiz on opt-in)
- "How long have you been in business?" (sign-up form field)
- Clicking a link labeled "I'm just getting started" vs. "I've been doing this for years"

This is the most reliable data. When people tell you where they are, believe them.

**How to collect it:**
- Ask one diagnostic question at opt-in: "What best describes you?" with 3–4 options
- Use a short welcome survey sent within 24 hours of subscribing
- Embed segmentation links in your emails ("Click here if this applies to you")

### 2. Behavioral Data (What They Do)
Actions subscribers take reveal their intent and interest level:
- Which emails they open
- Which links they click
- Which pages they visit on your site
- Whether they've purchased or not
- How long since they last engaged

Dunn uses behavioral data to build "engagement scores" — a proxy for how interested and ready a subscriber is. High open rate + multiple clicks on sales-adjacent content = warm lead. Six months of silence = re-engagement sequence needed.

### 3. Inferred Data (What You Deduce)
Combining explicit + behavioral to make reasonable inferences about subscriber needs.

Example: A subscriber who opted in to a lead magnet about "getting your first client" and has only clicked links in beginner-level posts is probably a newer consultant, even if they never told you that directly.

Inferred data should be held loosely — it's hypothesis, not fact. But it's useful for sending the right content when you don't have better signal.

## The RightMessage Approach: Website + Email Personalization

RightMessage was built by Dunn to solve a specific frustration: your website shows the same homepage to everyone, even though every visitor is at a different stage.

The core idea: just as email can be segmented, so can your website. A visitor who has already joined your email list should see a different CTA than a first-time visitor. A returning customer should see different social proof than a cold prospect.

**The personalization sequence:**
1. Segment the subscriber on first contact (opt-in quiz, survey)
2. Tag them in your email platform (ConvertKit, Drip, ActiveCampaign, etc.)
3. When they return to your website, show them personalized content based on their segment
4. As they engage over time, update their segment based on new behavior

This creates a continuous loop: your website feeds your email list, your email list informs your website, and both get sharper over time.

## The 5-Email Welcome Sequence: Segmentation in Action

The welcome sequence is where most coaches and consultants leave money on the table. They send a generic "thanks for subscribing" email and move on. Dunn's approach uses the welcome sequence as a segmentation engine.

**Email 1 (Day 0): The warm welcome + diagnostic question**
Confirm their subscribe, deliver what you promised, and immediately ask the diagnostic question: "Quick question — what's your biggest challenge right now with [topic]?"

Give 3–4 specific options. Each option links to a different URL that triggers a different tag in your email platform.

**Email 2 (Day 1): Relevant content based on their answer**
If they clicked "I'm struggling to find clients," send your best content on client acquisition. If they clicked "I don't know what to charge," send your pricing content.

If they didn't click anything, send your most popular/highest-value piece and watch which links they engage with.

**Email 3 (Day 3): Their specific problem, your framework**
Go deeper on their stated problem. Show that you understand the nuances. This is not a pitch — it's a demonstration that you are the right guide for where they are.

**Email 4 (Day 5): Social proof relevant to their situation**
A case study or testimonial from someone who had their exact problem and got the outcome they want. "Sarah was in your position six months ago. Here's what changed."

**Email 5 (Day 7): The offer (or the next step)**
Now you can make an offer — but it should be tied to their segment. A beginner gets offered your foundational course or audit. An advanced practitioner gets offered your premium retainer or intensive.

## Tags: The Infrastructure of Segmentation

Tags are the technical mechanism that makes segmentation work. Every email platform (ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, Drip) supports tagging.

A practical tagging system for a coach or consultant:

| Tag | What Triggers It |
|---|---|
| `stage: beginner` | Opted in via beginner-focused lead magnet |
| `stage: intermediate` | Clicked "I've been doing this 1–3 years" |
| `stage: advanced` | Self-identified or has high engagement + prior purchase |
| `interest: pricing` | Clicked pricing-related links in 2+ emails |
| `interest: clients` | Clicked client-acquisition content |
| `buyer: [product name]` | Purchased a specific product |
| `warm-lead` | Visited sales page 2+ times |
| `churned` | 90+ days with no opens |

With this tagging system, you can create broadcast emails that only go to subscribers with specific tags — meaning every email you send reaches only the people for whom it's relevant.

## Practical Application: The Consultant's Segmentation Stack

For a coaching or consulting business starting from scratch:

**Phase 1 (Month 1):** Add one diagnostic question to your opt-in form. Three options. Tag based on their choice. This alone puts you ahead of 90% of email marketers.

**Phase 2 (Month 2):** Build a 5-email welcome sequence that branches based on Phase 1 tags. The segmented version can be written in a week; the returns compound for years.

**Phase 3 (Month 3+):** Add behavioral tags — track which email content drives clicks, and tag subscribers based on demonstrated interest. Use this to determine who gets pitched what offer.

**The goal:** by Month 3, you should know (for each subscriber) what their primary challenge is, how long they've been in business, what content they're most interested in, and whether they've purchased. That's enough to send emails that feel like they were written for one person — not broadcast to thousands.
