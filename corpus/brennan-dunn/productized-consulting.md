# Productized Consulting: Fixed-Scope, Fixed-Price Offers

## What Is Productized Consulting?

Productized consulting is the practice of taking your expertise and packaging it into a defined, repeatable service with a fixed scope, fixed price, and a clear deliverable — rather than billing by the hour or negotiating a custom scope for every client.

The distinction matters: traditional consulting bills for *time*. Productized consulting sells an *outcome*. The client knows exactly what they're getting, what it will cost, and when it will be delivered. You know exactly what you're delivering, how long it will take, and how much you'll earn.

Nick Disabato, a pioneer in this model, built Draft Revise — a subscription service that tests and optimizes clients' websites on a recurring monthly or quarterly basis. Fixed deliverable. Fixed price. Recurring revenue. No scope creep, no hourly haggling.

---

## Why Productize?

**For the consultant:**
- Eliminates scope creep — the defined deliverable is the boundary
- Easier to sell: clients don't need to understand your process, just your outcome
- Scalable: once the process is documented, you can replicate it with less energy
- Commands premium pricing: you're selling a result, not your time
- Creates predictable monthly revenue vs. feast-or-famine project cycles

**For the client:**
- Clear ROI calculation upfront ("I'm spending $X to get Y")
- No billing surprises
- Lower perceived risk: they know what they're buying

## The Four Elements of a Strong Productized Offer

### 1. A Specific Problem You Solve
Not "I help businesses with their marketing." Instead: "I help B2B SaaS companies get their first 100 email subscribers using content-led SEO."

The tighter the problem, the easier it is to sell, because the client immediately recognizes themselves in it.

### 2. A Defined Client Profile
Productized consulting only works when you've identified exactly who it's for. Nick Disabato didn't sell website optimization to "anyone with a website" — he targeted companies with existing traffic who needed conversion improvement. Specificity removes self-qualification friction.

### 3. A Clear Deliverable
What does the client *receive*? A 12-page strategic roadmap? Six email templates? A conversion audit report with three prioritized recommendations? The deliverable must be concrete enough that both parties can agree it's been delivered.

### 4. A Fixed Price
The price is non-negotiable. This is the whole point. A variable price signals that your value is negotiable. A fixed price signals confidence and system. It also attracts clients who value the outcome over price-shopping the input.

## The Paid Discovery Session (Roadmapping)

One of the most powerful productized offers — and often the gateway into larger engagements — is the **paid discovery session**, also called a roadmap or paid proposal.

Here's the insight: most consultants give away their best thinking for free during the sales process. A paid discovery session flips this. The client pays for the strategy before any implementation work begins.

**How it works:**
1. Client pays a fixed fee (typically $500–$6,000+ depending on seniority) for a defined diagnostic session
2. You conduct deep interviews, review their situation, and produce a strategic roadmap document
3. The roadmap outlines the problem, the recommended solution, the implementation plan, and the expected outcome
4. Client can implement themselves, hire someone else, or hire you — but either way you've been paid for your expertise

Laura Elizabeth, co-owner of Double Your Freelancing, charges upward of **$6,000 for a single roadmap** session. The insight: clients don't question the price because the value of the diagnosis is obvious. A carpenter charges to assess your project before quoting the job. A doctor charges for the consultation. Why should a consultant give away the diagnosis for free?

**Why this converts:**
- Filters out unserious buyers
- Clients who pay for discovery are significantly more likely to proceed to implementation
- You stop investing unpaid hours in proposals that don't close
- It positions you as an advisor, not a vendor

## How to Build Your First Productized Offer

**Step 1: Audit your past client work.**
List every project you've completed. Note: what was the client's core problem? What was the outcome you delivered? What did they actually care about?

**Step 2: Find the pattern.**
Look for two or three recurring problem types. These are your productized offer candidates.

**Step 3: Define the deliverable.**
What is the tangible thing the client receives? Write it out in one sentence a client could understand.

**Step 4: Price it on value, not time.**
How much is solving this problem worth to the client? If your roadmap helps a client identify a $200K revenue leak, $3,000 for the roadmap is a no-brainer. Price at 5–15% of expected client value.

**Step 5: Test it with the next three prospects.**
Don't build a fancy sales page first. Describe the offer verbally in your next discovery call and see if it lands. Iterate before you invest in infrastructure.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Making it too broad.** "Strategy consulting for businesses" is not a product. "Email funnel audit for coaches with 1,000+ subscribers" is.

**Mistake 2: Underpricing to reduce risk.** Low prices signal low value. A $297 roadmap makes clients wonder what they're getting. A $2,000 roadmap signals serious expertise.

**Mistake 3: Building a productized offer for work you hate.** The repeatability only benefits you if you can execute it consistently with high quality. Pick something you're genuinely good at.

**Mistake 4: Skipping the qualifier.** Nick Disabato is explicit that his service is not for beginners — meaning, he only takes on clients who already have traffic and existing customers. The qualifier isn't exclusion; it's quality control.

## Application for a Coaching/Consulting Business

If you run a coaching or consulting practice, your productized offers might look like:

- **Business Diagnostic Session** ($1,500): 2-hour deep-dive + written report with three prioritized action items
- **90-Day Sprint** ($4,000/month): Fixed scope — weekly calls, email access, defined deliverables per sprint
- **Authority Content Package** ($3,000): Four LinkedIn articles + one lead magnet written in your voice, based on your frameworks

The goal: when someone asks "what do you offer?" you have a concrete answer, a concrete price, and a concrete outcome. That's productized consulting.
