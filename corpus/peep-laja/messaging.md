## The B2B Messaging Crisis

Most B2B companies have a messaging problem:
- They use jargon buyers don't recognize
- They focus on features, not outcomes
- Their homepage says the same thing as every competitor
- They write copy that their team loves but buyers don't care about

**Peep built Wynter** specifically to solve this — a platform to test B2B messaging with real target buyers before publishing it.

## What Good B2B Messaging Does

1. **Resonates immediately** — target buyer reads it and thinks "this is for me"
2. **Differentiates** — clearly different from how competitors talk
3. **Drives action** — creates enough desire/urgency to take a next step
4. **Builds trust** — specific, credible, not generic claims

## The Hierarchy of Messaging

**Company Narrative** (Who we are, why we exist, what we believe)
↓
**Positioning Statement** (Internal: how we're positioned vs. alternatives)
↓
**Value Proposition** (External: the core promise we make to buyers)
↓
**Key Messages** (3-5 supporting claims that back up the value prop)
↓
**Proof Points** (Specific evidence: case studies, stats, testimonials)
↓
**Copy** (The actual words on the website, ads, emails)

Most companies write copy without building the foundation. This is why messaging fails.

## Peep's Messaging Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness
"If you have to explain your clever headline, it failed."

Buyers scan, not read. If your message isn't clear in 3 seconds:
- They bounce from your website
- They don't read your email
- They don't remember your ad

### 2. Specific Over Generic
Generic: "We help companies grow"
Specific: "B2B SaaS companies add 2-3x more pipeline in 90 days with our outbound system"

Specific claims are:
- More believable
- More memorable
- More likely to resonate with the exact buyer you want

### 3. Outcome Over Feature
Buyers don't buy features — they buy outcomes.
- Don't say: "AI-powered prospecting tool"
- Do say: "Fill your pipeline with qualified leads without hiring more SDRs"

### 4. Differentiated Over Me-Too
Every claim you make that could also appear on a competitor's website is a wasted claim. Audit your messaging:
- Which of our claims is uniquely ours?
- What can we say that competitors can't credibly say?

### 5. Validated Over Assumed
Internal teams are biased. Peep's solution: test messaging with real buyers.
- Does this resonate with our ICP?
- Does this feel different from alternatives?
- Does this create urgency or desire?

**Wynter** enables this by providing panels of B2B buyers who give direct feedback on messaging in real-time.

## The Messaging Audit (Peep's Process)

**Step 1**: Collect all current messaging (website, ads, sales decks, email templates)

**Step 2**: Compare to top 5 competitors — what's the same? What's different?

**Step 3**: Interview 10-20 target buyers — what language do THEY use? What do they care about?

**Step 4**: Identify gaps between your messaging and buyer language

**Step 5**: Rewrite messaging using buyer language around buyer outcomes

**Step 6**: Test the new messaging with actual buyers before publishing

## Messaging by Channel

**Website Homepage**: Clearest possible statement of value for target ICP. Answer "Is this for me?" in 5 seconds.

**Landing Pages**: Single offer, single audience, no distractions. Match the message to the traffic source.

**Email**: Subject line = promise. Body = delivery. CTA = clear single action.

**Sales Deck**: Tell a story (problem → solution → proof → offer), don't feature dump.

**LinkedIn Ads**: Pattern interrupt headline, specific problem/outcome, proof element, clear CTA.
