## Core Ideas & Frameworks

### Evidence-Based Marketing
Peep is the leading voice for **running marketing like a scientist**: form hypotheses, run controlled tests, measure results, update your approach.

"Marketing without measurement is just hope."

### The Positioning-Messaging Gap
One of Peep's central arguments: most B2B companies can position correctly but communicate poorly. Positioning is about what you ARE. Messaging is how you COMMUNICATE that to different audiences. Getting both right is the work.

### Competitive Differentiation
Peep's framework: you can only win on 3 dimensions — **brand, product, or price**. Most companies try to be good at all three and end up average at all of them.

"If you're not different in a way that matters to your buyer, you are competing on price by default."

Real differentiation requires:
1. Knowing exactly who your best customers are
2. Understanding what they actually care about (not what you think they care about)
3. Identifying where your product genuinely outperforms alternatives
4. Communicating that delta clearly, specifically, and repeatedly

### Win-Loss Analysis
Peep's evidence-based approach to understanding why you win and lose deals:
- Interview recent wins AND losses (not just wins)
- Losses tell you more than wins
- The buying decision is almost always made before sales talks to them
- Your message is working or not working BEFORE a prospect enters your pipeline

### Conversion Rate Optimization
CRO is not just "A/B testing buttons." It's a systematic process:
1. **Research** — understand what visitors actually experience (heatmaps, session recordings, surveys)
2. **Analysis** — identify friction points, leaks, and opportunities
3. **Hypothesis** — form specific, testable hypotheses about what to change and why
4. **Experiment** — run controlled tests
5. **Learn** — whether the test wins or loses, extract the insight
6. **Iterate** — use learnings to build the next test

The most underutilized CRO tool: asking customers why they bought.

### SaaS Marketing Framework
Key insight: **most SaaS companies spend 90% on acquisition and 10% on retention, but retention is where the economics are**. Churn kills growth.

Priority order:
1. Nail the core product value (retention foundation)
2. Nail messaging that reaches buyers where they are
3. Nail the acquisition channels that match your buyer's research behavior
4. Optimize the conversion path
