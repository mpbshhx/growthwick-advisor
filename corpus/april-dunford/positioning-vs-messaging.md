# Storytelling vs Positioning — April Dunford

## Can You Differentiate Storytelling vs Positioning?

Easy to confuse the two — but they are not the same thing.

Many startups try to use a storytelling exercise to get clarity on positioning. Starting with storytelling without working on positioning first is dangerous.

## Structured Storytelling Needs Good Inputs

Good structured storytelling approaches (like *Building a Story Brand* by Donald Miller) start with assumptions:
- Tight definition of target customer
- What value the offering delivers to that buyer
- Value is differentiated from competitors
- You know exactly who those competitors are

## You Can't Brainstorm Your Way to Good Positioning

Many companies (especially startups) don't know the exact problem they solve, or the exact profile of their ideal customer. Worse: they think they know, but they're wrong.

You can run an internal brainstorm and produce a compelling story — but also absolute bullcrap. Why? Because your inputs — the problem definition, customer profile, unique value — came from an internal session, not from actual business results.

## What You Need First: Positioning Inputs

Before crafting a story, you need clarity on:
1. Real competitive alternatives to your solution
2. Unique features you have
3. Differentiated value those features drive
4. Segments of customers that care a lot about that value
5. Market category where you win

These are the basic components of positioning. They must come BEFORE the story.

## Positioning First, Storytelling Next

**Stories that sell must be grounded in deep understanding of who loves your products best and why.**

- Positioning is NOT the result of a story
- Stories REQUIRE positioning as an input
- You cannot do it the other way around

You need a positioning process that gets you to the reality of who loves your stuff and why. Once you have that, you can make positioning sing using a great storytelling framework.

## The Hierarchy

1. **Positioning** (what market you intend to win and why you deserve to win it)
2. **Storytelling / Messaging** (how you communicate that positioning compellingly)
3. **Everything else** — taglines, branding, vision, brand story — are all downstream of positioning
