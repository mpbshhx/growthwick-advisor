# April Dunford — Positioning Expert Profile

## Who She Is

April Dunford is the world's foremost authority on B2B technology positioning. She is the author of **"Obviously Awesome: How to Nail Product Positioning so Customers Get It, Buy It, Love It"** (2019) and **"Sales Pitch: How to Craft a Story That Wins"** (2023).

She spent 25+ years as a marketing executive at a series of successful startups before pivoting to consulting and speaking. Her structured positioning methodology has been battle-tested with 200+ companies — from early-stage startups to some of the biggest companies in the world.

## Core Positioning

**Her tagline:** "Practical positioning that accelerates marketing and sales."

**The problem she solves:** Prospects are drowning in a sea of competitive choices. Companies struggle to help their best-fit customers intuitively understand the value of their products.

**Her definition of positioning:** Positioning defines how your product is different and better than alternatives for a particular set of customers. Strong positioning is the foundation of great marketing and sales — the starting point for messaging, branding, lead generation, and sales strategy.

## Core Framework: 10 Components of Positioning

1. Competitive alternatives (what do customers compare you to?)
2. Unique attributes (what features do you have that alternatives don't?)
3. Value (what value do those attributes enable for customers?)
4. Customer segments (who cares a lot about that value?)
5. Market category (what context makes the value obvious?)
6. Plus: Relevant trends
7-10. [Further components covered in Obviously Awesome]

**Key insight:** Most positioning work starts with the wrong assumptions. You need to work from competitive alternatives forward — not from product features back.

## Methodological Signature Moves

- **"What do you do when customers don't know what category you're in?"** — she has a framework for positioning new markets and categories

- **Crossing the Chasm meets Jobs-to-be-Done** — she bridges Geoffrey Moore's category thinking with modern product strategy
