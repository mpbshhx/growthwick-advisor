# 74% of Professionals Call AI Essential But Their Companies Lag Behind

By Cathy McPhillips | May 27, 2026

There's a moment in every technology cycle when a tool stops being a competitive edge and starts becoming essential to work. For AI in B2B marketing, that time has arrived.

## The Data (2026 State of AI for Business Report)

- **74%** of respondents said AI is "critically important" or "very important" to their success over the next 12 months
- Survey: 2,100+ professionals, 48% at B2B organizations
- "That's nearing about as close to a consensus on a survey question as you can get." — Taylor Radey, Director of Research at SmarterX

## From Experiment to Expectation

"You're starting to see the sentiment shift. And these are AI-forward people, the people seeing the tools every day, who are realizing the impact." — Paul Roetzer, CEO of SmarterX and Marketing AI Institute

## The Gap: Employees vs. Organizations

- **53%** of professionals are now in the Integration or Transformation phases of AI adoption
- But only **25%** of their organizations have reached the Scaling phase
- Nearly **47%** of organizations still stuck in pilot mode

"This is not a knowledge gap. The people inside these organizations know what AI can do. But the organizations themselves haven't built the infrastructure to operationalize AI and take full advantage of what their own employees are already doing on their own." — Taylor Radey

## Why This Matters for B2B Marketers

Your team is almost certainly ahead of your company. Your CEO is likely ahead of your team. Connecting the key elements of a successful AI-forward company — roadmap, governance, training, dedicated time — determines whether individual progress turns into real business progress.

The data settles it: Is AI optional? Individual workers clearly say it is not. The question now is whether organizations will catch up.

## What Can B2B Marketers Do Now?

1. **Create space for AI learning and experimentation.** Employees want guidance, training, and time to develop AI skills — not just access to tools.

2. **Move beyond isolated pilots.** Identify high-impact workflows where AI can drive measurable value across content, research, operations, customer insights, and personalization.

3. **Develop governance before scale creates risk.** Clear policies around data usage, brand standards, legal considerations, and human oversight. Waiting until AI is fully embedded makes governance harder.

4. **Build an AI roadmap tied to business outcomes.** Align AI with goals: efficiency, revenue growth, customer experience, competitive advantage.

5. **Pay attention to internal momentum.** The companies that move fastest will likely be the ones that listen to the people closest to the work and empower them to shape adoption.
