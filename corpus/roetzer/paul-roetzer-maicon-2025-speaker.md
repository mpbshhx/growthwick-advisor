# How to Prepare Knowledge Workers for an AI-Powered Future with Paul Roetzer [MAICON 2025 Speaker Series]

By Cathy McPhillips | October 9, 2025

Paul Roetzer has spent his career at the intersection of marketing and innovation. Co-host of The Artificial Intelligence Show, co-author of Marketing Artificial Intelligence, and founder of MAICON.

What began as an experiment within his former agency — a simple blog and newsletter — has grown into the Marketing AI Institute, a global hub for AI education with more than 100,000 subscribers. Under the Institute's parent company SmarterX.

## Session: Prepare for Your Own "Move37" Moment

In 2016, Google's AlphaGo stunned the world by defeating champion Lee Sedol in the ancient game of Go — remembered for "Move 37." In that moment, Sedol realized AI had surpassed even his most creative instincts.

Paul believes each of us will soon face our own "Move 37" moment at work.

"It might be micro-moments, like AI that can write better or create more engaging artwork. Regardless, there is a reasonable chance you're going to have that moment where you realize AI will do that thing better."

"So, we need to prepare for this moment and be optimistic about what we can do from there."

Our response shouldn't be to fear these moments, but to prepare:
- Explore today's models
- Understand what's on the horizon
- Reframe what it means to be human in an AI-powered world

## AI's Untapped Potential — The Key Lesson

"Many professionals still don't fully grasp how AI can tackle deep research and complex problem-solving. Because of this, many professionals aren't prepared for, or understand, what is truly possible."

"Once you do, it will change your entire perspective on the future of work."

AI shouldn't just influence your career decisions; it should shape how you think about hiring and team planning. How can you make the right "people" decisions if you don't understand what AI can already do?

## AI Disruption Is Coming

"AI is going to be way more disruptive than most people understand or want to believe."

Leaders who already are applying AI will have a big advantage. Those who refuse to embrace it will experience a period of intense disruption.

OpenAI is responding through the launch of its AI hiring platform, scheduled for 2026.

The changes ahead can be unnerving, but they also offer a big opportunity for professionals to rethink careers, pivot into new opportunities, and reimagine what's possible.

## MAICON Session Description

"What Sedol experienced in Go — his 'Move 37' — is coming for every knowledge worker, in every industry. AI is on a near-term path to surpass the average human at most cognitive tasks. And many AI researchers, including some leaders of Google DeepMind, believe AI systems will achieve superhuman levels at all cognitive tasks (i.e. better than the best humans) by the end of the decade."
