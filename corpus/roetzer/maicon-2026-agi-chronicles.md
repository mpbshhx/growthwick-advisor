# Kevin Roose at MAICON 2026: The AGI Chronicles

**Marketing AI Institute | May 2026 | By Cathy McPhillips**

*Note: This article captures MAICON 2026 keynote context — reflects Paul Roetzer's curation of the most consequential AI thinkers for the marketing industry.*

## About Kevin Roose

Kevin Roose is an award-winning technology columnist for The New York Times and the best-selling author of:
- *Futureproof* — a guide to surviving the technological future (2021, re-released 2023)
- *Young Money* — chronicles of junior Wall Street investment bankers post-2008
- *The Unlikely Disciple* — memoir from going undercover at Liberty University

Host of New York Times podcasts: **Hard Fork** (weekly tech chat with Casey Newton) and **Rabbit Hole** (internet influence on beliefs and behavior).

## The Keynote: The AGI Chronicles

Roose's core belief: **We are living through one of the most consequential technological projects in history.**

Every week: crazy technical breakthroughs, world-changing policy debates. Yet in an industry run on auto-deleting Signal chats, house parties, and Twitter anons — nobody seems to be writing anything down.

> "Imagine if we had no contemporaneous records of the Apollo program, or the Manhattan Project. Think of how impoverished our collective understanding would be if, when we're living in the fully-automated post-AGI utopia, no record of how it happened, when and how the key decisions were made, or who saw it coming first."

## Why This Matters for Marketers (Roetzer's Framing)

MAICON 2026 brought together 50+ AI and business leaders navigating the AI shift. Roetzer's thesis: marketers need to understand the AGI transition not as a distant event but as a current reality reshaping their profession NOW.

The "Futureproof" framework: work WITH AI and automation rather than against it. Survive by being the humans machines can't replace.
