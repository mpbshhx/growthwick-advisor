# The Law of Uneven AI Distribution

By Paul Roetzer, Marketing AI Institute

## The Law

**The value you gain from AI, and how quickly and consistently that value is realized, is directly proportional to your understanding of, access to, and acceptance of the technology.**

---

## The Core Premise

The impact and benefits of AI will be unevenly distributed to individuals, companies, and industries. In some cases by your own choice, in others by institutional design.

Examples:
- Financial services companies blocking employee ChatGPT access
- Educational systems struggling to adapt curriculum to generative AI
- Individuals refusing to install interesting AI apps due to data trust concerns

This uneven distribution creates dramatic differences in how much value people extract from AI.

---

## Benefits of AI When Adopted

**Personal life:**
- Personalization
- Convenience
- Abundance
- Fulfillment
- Happiness

**Professional life:**
- Efficiency
- Productivity
- Creativity
- Predictability
- Success

---

## 3 Factors That Influence Distribution of Benefits

### 1. Understanding of AI

Three stages of understanding:
- **Awareness:** You know AI exists and has business applications (many leaders missed this pre-ChatGPT)
- **Comprehension:** Learning what AI is, how it works, exploring use cases and technologies
- **Competency:** Running pilot projects, assessing vendors, seeing lifts in productivity, advancing skills continuously

Most business leaders were "blissfully unaware of the impending impact of AI before the ChatGPT moment in November 2022. Now everyone is racing to solve for AI in business, but lacking the talent, vision and strategy to execute."

### 2. Access to AI

Three key factors determining access:
- **Cost:** Can you afford the solution?
- **Governance:** Can you use AI within organizational or regulatory constraints?
- **Gatekeepers:** Technology companies and research labs control when and how more advanced AI is released to the public

### 3. Acceptance of AI

Even if you understand and have access to AI, acceptance is the final variable that determines value extracted.

Key acceptance challenges:
- **Resistance to change:** Especially for creative professionals, accepting AI's evolving role in content generation is difficult
- **Data tradeoff willingness:** You must be willing to give up the data needed to realize AI's key benefits
- **Tolerance for constant change:** AI capabilities expand continuously — acceptance requires ongoing adaptation
- **Awareness of risks:** Must accept potential downsides from known and unknown risks

---

## Downsides of AI Distribution

Even early adopters face real risks:
- As more personal data is collected to drive AI, the incentive to steal that data grows — identity and security risk increases
- Knowledge work will be disrupted — there should be net positive impact on jobs over the coming decade, but the transition won't be smooth
- Bad actors will use AI to spread misinformation, disinformation, deepfakes, and propaganda — affecting business, politics, and society

---

## Actions You Can Take

1. **Be curious and explore AI.** Seek knowledge in whatever format you prefer — books, podcasts, online courses. Understanding is essential to maximize the positive impact.

2. **Determine your data tradeoffs.** Figure out what data you, your family, and your company are willing to give up in exchange for convenience, personalization, and other benefits.

3. **Balance innovation with responsibility.** Consider your company's policies and principles around AI usage — ensure a balance between innovation and the responsible application of AI technologies.
