# AI Costs Are Outpacing Marketing Budgets — How to Strategize

**Marketing AI Institute | June 2026 | By Mike Kaput**

## The Problem

Corporate America is starting to ration AI. Some enterprises have burned through their entire annual AI budget in just a few months. Others have watched AI spending double or triple with little warning.

For marketers running AI tools for content, campaigns, reporting, or outreach: the cost dynamics have shifted dramatically. Most budgets set in late 2025 were not built for AI agents.

## What's Driving the Spike: Tokens and Agents

Unlike a chatbot that answers a single question, **AI agents work through tasks in multiple steps** — and every step consumes tokens.

- An agent drafting a content brief, researching prospects, or pulling campaign data isn't making one request. It's making dozens, sometimes hundreds.
- Goldman Sachs (May 2026): "Agentic AI requires a lot of tokens because many queries are repeated in sequence. It's like taking a simple chatbot request and blowing it up 10-fold, 20-fold, 50-fold."
- Token consumption is projected to multiply **24x between 2026 and 2030**

## Marketing Teams' AI Use Cases (High Token Consumers)

- Content creation
- Personalization at scale
- Campaign analysis
- Social copy
- Email sequences
- SEO research

## The Visibility Problem

Most marketers don't know:
- How many tokens they're consuming
- What's driving the bill
- Which work produced the most value per token

## How Marketing Leaders Should Respond

### Don't cut access — strategize instead

**1. Audit before you restrict**
- Understand where tokens are actually going
- Which workflows consume the most? Which produce measurable marketing outcomes?
- Data should drive decisions, not raw usage numbers

**2. Match the model to the task**
- The most capable (and expensive) model isn't always right
- A frontier reasoning model is overkill for a social media post or blog
- Build a culture of deliberate model selection to reduce costs without reducing quality

**3. Measure AI output, not token usage**
- Token usage is the wrong KPI
- What matters: time saved, content produced, pipeline influenced
- Connect AI activity to valuable marketing outcomes to justify spend and make smarter decisions

## Key Principle from Paul Roetzer

> "The marketing leaders who navigate budgets well will have a solid strategy behind it."
