# Andrew Yang at MAICON 2026: How AI Can Build a Future that Works for Everyone

**Marketing AI Institute | May 2026 | By Cathy McPhillips**

*Note: Reflects Paul Roetzer's MAICON curation — themes of AI + workforce + economy that Roetzer sees as essential context for marketing leaders.*

## The Core Challenge

Every leader trying to navigate AI faces a similar challenge: How to keep pace with a technology moving faster than our institutions, our policies, and our economic framework.

## Yang's Central Argument

> "I'm personally very confident that AI is going to eat through many American jobs. Just the other day I spoke to someone who said they used to have 15 designers suggesting various ideas for graphics and clothing. They recently fired them all because now they use AI for the same task. A lot of creative work is being changed to utilize AI, which often means fewer workers. What 4 years ago was hypothetical is now immediate." — Andrew Yang, 2024

## The Human-Centered Economy Framework (MAICON 2026 Keynote)

Yang's keynote covered three areas:

### 1. How exponential technologies are transforming the economy faster than our systems can adapt
- AI disruption is no longer hypothetical — it's immediate
- Drawing from his 2020 presidential campaign platform that put AI at the center
- UBI, data as a property right, and modern safety nets as responses

### 2. The urgent need to rethink traditional economic models
- Universal Basic Income as a structural response to AI displacement
- Data as a property right for citizens
- Building a modern safety net for displaced workers

### 3. The need for optimism, practicality, and empathy in the age of AI
> "Left to its own devices, there will be some major downsides. If we want more of the good and less of the bad, it will be up to us and our leaders." — Andrew Yang

## Why It Matters for Marketing Leaders

Yang is not anti-technology. He is clear about AI's potential to improve healthcare, advance scientific breakthroughs, and expand human capacity.

But he's focused on what happens when transformative technology outpaces the systems meant to govern it.

For leaders responsible for AI strategy, adoption, and governance:
- Workforce planning
- Vendor evaluation  
- Risk management
- Long-term positioning
