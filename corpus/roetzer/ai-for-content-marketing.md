# Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in Content Marketing

By Mike Kaput, Marketing AI Institute (co-authored with Paul Roetzer's team)

## The Core Premise

No matter what content you write or what stories your brand tells, there's a good chance AI can help you do it better. Content marketing is one of the top areas where AI can significantly increase revenue and reduce costs.

In MAII's 2021 State of Marketing AI Report, content marketing activities dominated the list of highest-rated use cases for AI:
- Content analysis
- Keyword selection
- Data-driven content creation
- Optimization and A/B testing
- Creating personalized content

---

## What AI Is and How It Influences Content Marketing

AI covers many different technologies: machine learning, computer vision, natural language processing (NLP), natural language generation (NLG), and more.

**NLP** = AI's ability to "read" human language
**NLG** = AI's ability to "write" or "speak" human language

The real magic: advanced systems improve themselves over time based on user inputs, rather than relying on a human programmer to update the rules.

**GPT evolution:** In 2019, OpenAI released GPT-2, which could write coherent paragraphs at scale. In 2020, GPT-3 followed — 175 billion parameters vs. GPT-2's 1.5 billion. By 2022, ChatGPT (GPT-4 series) made these capabilities mainstream. Today, AI can:
- Generate long-form content
- Build content strategy
- Personalize content at scale
- Optimize and recommend content

---

## What AI Skills Do Content Marketers Need?

**Skills more susceptible to automation:**
- Repetitive tasks (same steps, little variation)
- Data-driven tasks (using data to produce outputs)
- Predictive tasks (making predictions about outcomes)

**Skills content marketers need to thrive:**
1. **AI knowledge and understanding** — Know the types of AI technology and the opportunities/challenges they present
2. **Data literacy** — Understand how data impacts AI adoption (you don't need a data science degree, but you need to think clearly about data)
3. **Use case fluency** — Know how AI can actually help content marketing so you can test tools intelligently

## AI Tools by Category

### For Content Production
- **AI writing assistants** — Can draft email subject lines, ad copy, and increasingly longer-form content
- **MarketMuse** — AI-driven assistant for content strategy; shows what terms to target, surfaces topic gaps, can draft initial content outlines
- **rasa.io** — AI platform for personalized smart newsletters

### For Content Intelligence
- **Crayon** — Uses AI for competitive intelligence; tracks how competitor websites and messaging change over time
- **Cobomba** — AI-powered content intelligence; measures content metrics over time, offers smart recommendations

### For Content Optimization and Recommendation
- **BrightEdge** — SEO and content performance marketing; AI for search intent discovery and content strategy
- **PathFactory** — AI-powered content recommendation; hyper-personalizes the B2B buyer journey with relevant content at each stage

## The Broader Implication (Roetzer's View)

This article predates the ChatGPT era but establishes the foundational lens: AI's role in content marketing isn't to replace content marketers — it's to make them dramatically more effective by handling the repetitive, data-driven, and predictive work.

The practitioners who invest early in AI understanding, data literacy, and use-case fluency will build a compounding advantage over those who don't.

This connects directly to Roetzer's Law of Uneven AI Distribution: the value you extract from AI is proportional to your understanding of, access to, and acceptance of it.
