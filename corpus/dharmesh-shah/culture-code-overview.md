"It's not a list of rules. It's a description of who we are."

## HEART Framework (Core Values)

| Letter | Value | What It Means |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| H | Humble | No room for ego. We check egos at the door. |
| E | Empathetic | We think about others — employees, customers, community |
| A | Adaptable | We are constantly learning, iterating, changing |
| R | Remarkable | We aspire to do work worth talking about |
| T | Transparent | We default to sharing information, not hoarding it |

## Core Beliefs

**"We obsess over customers, not competitors."**
HubSpot's north star is helping customers grow. Competitors are irrelevant if you're genuinely solving for the customer.

**"We solve for the customer."**
Every decision is run through this lens: does this solve for the customer? Not "does this solve for the business?" — they believed if you solve for the customer, the business follows.

**"Culture is a competitive advantage."**
You can copy products. You can't copy culture. A remarkable culture = remarkable talent = remarkable product = remarkable growth.

**"Defaulting to transparency."**
HubSpot shares information broadly — financials, strategy, decisions. The assumption: when people have context, they make better decisions. Hidden information creates politics.

**"Autonomy with accountability."**
Employees are trusted to make decisions. They're held accountable for outcomes. Not micromanaged on process.

## On Hiring

**"We hire for HEART, then skills."**
You can train skills. You can't train character.

**"We don't just accept diversity — we seek it."**
Diverse teams make better decisions. HubSpot actively recruited diverse talent because homogeneous teams have blind spots.

**"Past performance is not a predictor."**
HubSpot valued trajectory and raw potential over pedigree. Where are you going? Not where have you been?

## On Feedback

**eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score) every quarter.**
Every employee asked: "On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend HubSpot as a place to work?"

All comments read. All comments acted upon. The eNPS number tracked publicly within HubSpot like a business metric.

The signal: employee engagement is a leading indicator of customer happiness, which is a leading indicator of business health.

## On Failure

**"We believe in failing forward."**
Trying things and failing is not just acceptable — it's required. Teams that never fail aren't taking enough risks.

**"No blame, only learning."**
Post-mortems focus on systemic issues, not individuals. "What did we learn?" not "whose fault was this?"

## On Results vs. Hours

**"We value results, not hours."**
HubSpot was early on this. Output matters. Where and when you do the work matters less.

**"Autonomy matters. Accountability matters more."**

## On Growth and Learning

**"We invest in employee development."**
HubSpot pioneered the culture of continuous learning in SaaS: books, courses, conference budgets, learning days.

**"We want to be a stepping stone, not a destination."**
If HubSpot makes you better and you eventually leave for a great opportunity, that's a win. Alumni who love HubSpot become advocates, partners, and recruiters.

## Why This Matters for Growthwick

The HubSpot Culture Code is one of the defining documents of modern SaaS company culture. Its key marketing lessons:

1. **Culture IS a marketing asset** — it attracts talent, which builds product, which serves customers
2. **Transparency builds trust** — both internally and externally with customers
3. **eNPS is a growth metric** — engaged employees produce better customer experiences
4. **HEART over skills** — the people you hire define your ceiling
5. **Solve for the customer, always** — the guiding principle that made HubSpot a category leader
