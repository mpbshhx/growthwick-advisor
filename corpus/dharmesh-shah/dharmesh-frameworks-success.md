# Simple Frameworks For Success — Dharmesh Shah on My First Million Podcast

My First Million Podcast, Episode #197 with Sam Parr and Shaan Puri | July 8, 2021

## Dharmesh's Background

- Co-founder/CTO of HubSpot (NYSE: HUBS)
- Sold first company for $10-15M, went to MIT Sloan for grad school, met Brian Halligan there → HubSpot
- Has zero direct reports at HubSpot (by design, for 15 years)
- Net worth majority in HubSpot shares (publicly traded, so public information)
- Angel investor: Coinbase and others
- Owns many seven-figure domain names
- Plans to donate 90%+ of his wealth

## Framework #1: Time Value — Put an Hourly Rate on Your Time

Early in his career, Dharmesh got bumped to $125/hour as an engineer in Birmingham, Alabama in the 1990s. He decided: "If there's anything I've been doing with my time that could be done by spending less than $125 an hour, I'm being an idiot."

He's carried that idea forward, always asking: What value would I place on my time? What is occupying my time that I can delegate, even at a premium?

"Most people don't value their time enough, and don't think about it objectively enough. Put a number on it and then ask yourself: 'What are the things I'm doing with my time that are not worth that?'"

**The math across his career:** ~60 hours/week × 30 years → average hourly rate of ~$10,000/hour (back-loaded). He would pay a premium above that to buy back a future hour.

## Framework #2: No Direct Reports + Three Things

Dharmesh's agreement with Brian Halligan when founding HubSpot:
- Brian is CEO (Dharmesh isn't good at being CEO, knows it, doesn't want to waste years getting merely "passably okay" at it)
- Dharmesh has zero direct reports, forever

His philosophy: "Take your strengths, whatever they are, and put all your energy into kind of amplifying those strengths and getting really, really good at that thing, and don't worry about your weaknesses all that much."

He whittles his HubSpot work down to three things at a time:
- Changes every few years
- Has included: brand, culture, boldness (pushing org to take enough risk), product, platform
- When culture was on the list → spent 300 hours on the HubSpot Culture Code deck
- Obsesses over details in whatever he owns: reviewing copy line by line, not floating at 50,000 feet

## Framework #3: Money → Freedom, Not Stuff

Dharmesh grew up with a modest upbringing. Wasn't focused on money itself — was focused on freedom ("configuring the universe to my liking").

His money stages:
- **First $5M:** Had a "dramatic impact" — bought freedom, didn't have to work again, could invest
- **Beyond that:** Marginal utility decreases. Hard to spend more than a reasonable amount on food, experiences, things he actually enjoys
- **Atoms vs. bits:** Doesn't crave physical things (yacht, plane) because atoms require maintenance and mental overhead. Bits (software, ideas) don't.

**Philanthropic intent:** Plans to donate 90%+ with wife. Not building a multi-generational dynasty. "Working for my wife's foundation — that's essentially what it comes down to."

## Framework #4: Control Your Distribution

On why HubSpot acquired The Hustle: "The future of SaaS companies is going to be heading toward companies that control their own distribution versus renting audiences from other people."

"I'm a believer in companies controlling their destiny in terms of distribution versus constantly just buying audiences and renting them from someone else."

## Framework #5: Copywriting is Underrated

"Copywriting overall is one of the most underrated skills in entrepreneurship generally. People do not realize the amount of leverage you get by just spending 10, 20, 50 hours on it."

"The nice thing about it... it's measurable. You can objectively measure whether you got better at copywriting. You can look at your conversion rates or whatever numbers you're trying to move as a result of your words. It's a learnable skill."

Dharmesh attended Shaan Puri's copywriting course while running a $25B+ company.

## On HubSpot's Culture

HubSpot culture identity: "Kinder, gentler, humble, quieter kind of company." Not type-A. Not brash.

The Culture Code: Dharmesh spent 300 hours on it. It has accumulated over 5 million views on SlideShare. It shaped how a generation of SaaS companies think about values and culture.

HubSpot's HEART values: Humble, Empathetic, Adaptable, Remarkable, Transparent.
