# 7 Reasons Scale-Ups Earn Investments, According to HubSpot's Founder — Dharmesh Shah

## The Startup-to-Scaleup Transition

Every successful company starts with a single idea. Reaching startup-dom is not the end — it's when ideas begin to grow into something actionable, driven by intentional goals.

**Being a startup = treading water.** Constant motion to:
- Find product-market fit
- Drive early customer growth
- Build a baseline product

All at the same time. Stop moving = lose traction.

Investments = the first lifeboat from startup to scaleup.

## 7 Signs a Scaling Company is Worth Investing In

### 1. Evidence of Customer Happiness
- Look at NPS (Net Promoter Score) and customer satisfaction scores
- If results are green → company is making positive impact
- If results are red → investors take warning
- Also look at: low or decreasing churn
- Retention rates = first sign of fan favorite vs. stepping stone

### 2. Great People in Primary Roles
- In early startup years, sales/marketing/operations roles often understaffed — acceptable
- When scaling: these roles MUST be filled with great people (or a plan to hire them)
- The potential of a scale-up = the potential of the people dedicated to its growth
- At HubSpot: "Brian and I surrounded ourselves with people smarter than us. Those investments paid off, big time."

### 3. Stable and Sustainable Unit Economics
- No one expects impressive ROI immediately
- What matters: stability and sustainability
- **Rule:** Customer Lifetime Value should be 3x+ Customer Acquisition Cost
- This shows: customers are desirable AND you can retain them and gain value over time
- Scalability and sustainability are two sides of the same coin

### 4. Well-Articulated Company Culture
- Culture is embedded in every decision and every person in the company
- Encompasses: mission, vision, and values
- Company with "grit" (Angela Duckworth): passion + perseverance = startup success
- Culture should be written down (reference: HubSpot Culture Code — 128-slide deck)
- Culture provides stable jumping-off point for: recruitment, retention, alignment
- Better culture understanding → more efficient shared vision

### 5. Strong Focus on Creating Customer Value
- Companies that succeed: don't just delight sometimes, they consistently exceed expectations
- Must proactively create value, not just react to customer needs
- Best method: open dialogue between company and customers
- Questions to ask:
  - How is your team getting customer input and acting on it?
  - Who beyond support is focused on the customer?
  - Is there a customer success team?
  - Have you defined the voice of your customers?
  - Is the customer represented in decision-making?

### 6. Strong Strategic Planning Process + Product Prioritization
- Emphasis: there IS a process and mechanism — not that it's perfect
- Disorganized planning = impossible to scale
- Scaling = proactively setting up for future successes
- Clearly defined planning process signals to investors you're looking ahead
- Also evaluate: Are systems built for scaling? Will they be easy to upgrade?

### 7. Engaged Workforce
- Unhappy workforce = red flag for new hires and investors
- Must: regularly collect employee feedback AND act on it
- A handful of disengaged employees is typical; a collection of them is a trend
- High turnover = impossible to scale
- HubSpot: eNPS survey every quarter for all employees
- Read and evaluate every comment → constantly improve employee experience

## The Core Insight

Not every scaleup needs all 7 signs to be worthy of investment. But the more positive signals = higher odds of long-term success.

The "secret sauce" successful startups share: a clear customer focus, the right people, a culture worth belonging to, and systems that can scale.
