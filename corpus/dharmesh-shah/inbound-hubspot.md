# What Is Inbound Marketing? | HubSpot

## The Core Framework

Inbound marketing automation combines the inbound methodology with marketing automation technology to deliver the right content to the right person at the right time — without requiring teams to manually manage every interaction.

**The relationship:** Inbound marketing is the strategy. Automation is the engine that makes it possible to execute at scale.

Key stat from HubSpot's 2026 State of Marketing Report:
- 92% of marketers now use automation for data analysis and reporting
- 40% of marketing teams say their customer journeys are mostly or fully automated

## The Three-Stage Inbound Methodology

### 1. Attract Stage
Draw in the right audience with content that answers their questions and earns their attention.

**Automation tools:**
- Automated content scheduling and distribution (blog posts, social, email triggers)
- SEO and AEO Monitoring (HubSpot's AEO Grader evaluates AI search visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini)
- Ad Retargeting Workflows (re-engage visitors who viewed specific pages but didn't convert)

**HubSpot principle:** Automating the attract stage doesn't mean removing human element from content creation — it means removing friction from distribution. Content still requires original thinking, expertise, and unique point of view.

### 2. Engage Stage
Turn awareness into conversation through personalized, scalable engagement.

**Automation tools:**
- Lead Nurturing Workflows (drip campaigns triggered by specific actions: downloading a resource, visiting a pricing page, attending a webinar, engaging with an email)
- Behavioral Email Sequences (prospects who read three blog posts about data migration get a case study on implementation; leads who visited pricing page twice get a direct sales invitation)
- Smart Forms and Progressive Profiling (forms adapt based on what you already know — build rich profiles without overwhelming 12-field forms)
- Lead Scoring (assign scores based on demographic fit + behavioral engagement; when threshold hit, notify sales or route lead into sales sequence)

**HubSpot principle:** Most effective engagement automations aren't complicated. Start with one high-performing content offer, one email sequence, one lead scoring threshold. Get that loop working before adding complexity.

### 3. Delight Stage
Turn customers into advocates, renewals, and expansion revenue.

**Automation tools:**
- Onboarding Sequences (guide new customers through product setup and best practices in first 30/60/90 days)
- NPS and Satisfaction Surveys (trigger at key milestones — after onboarding, after support interaction, at renewal)
- Customer Feedback Loops (route positive feedback → review request campaigns; negative feedback → support escalation workflows)
- Upsell and Cross-sell Triggers (when existing customers engage with content about advanced features, surface relevant offers or route to account manager)

**Best for:** Teams with a solid attract-and-engage engine who struggle with retention, expansion, or generating referrals.

## The Flywheel Model

Inbound marketing creates compounding value:
1. Great content attracts the right audience
2. Smart engagement converts interested visitors to leads
3. Excellent delight turns customers into promoters who attract new visitors
4. The cycle repeats and compounds

## Why Automation Matters

Without automation: Resource-intensive, inconsistent, scales poorly
With automation: Scalable, personalized, repeatable, teams spend time on high-impact work instead of repetitive tasks
