## What is Voice of Customer (VOC) Data?

VOC data = the collective insights and feedback gathered directly from customers. Their desires, pain points, and words spoken straight from the heart.

**NOT just:** a pool of comments or thread of reviews.
**IS:** firsthand knowledge that tells you exactly what your audience thinks and feels about your product or service.

---

## Why VOC is the Foundation of Conversion Copywriting

- Serves as your roadmap to craft copy that resonates personally and viscerally
- Instead of guessing, VOC empowers you with exact words and phrases customers use
- Allows you to mirror their language and create deeper connection
- Bridges the gap between what you want to say and what your audience wants to hear

---

## How to Gather VOC Data

### Primary Research Methods
- **Surveys** — structured questions to capture candid opinions
- **Customer interviews** — deep dives into motivations, pain points, objections
- **Support ticket analysis** — questions customers ask reveal confusion and blockers

### Secondary Research Methods
- **Product reviews** — Amazon, G2, Capterra, app stores
- **Social media conversations** — Twitter threads, Reddit discussions, Facebook groups
- **Customer feedback channels** — NPS surveys, chat transcripts
- **Competitive reviews** — what do customers say about competitors?

### The Gold: Unsolicited Feedback
Comments customers leave without any prompt often hold the key to genuine insights. These spontaneous remarks reveal what matters most.

## The Research & Discovery Process (Before Writing Any Copy)

### Answer Two Critical Questions First:
1. **What do you need to say?**
2. **How should you say it?**

VOC data answers both.

### Step-by-Step Research Process

**1. Identify your "List"**
Who are you addressing? Website traffic, customers, subscribers, or a single individual?

**2. Define your "Offer"**
What value proposition are you presenting? What's the goal of your copy?

**3. Map your reader's Stage of Awareness**
- Unaware → Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware → Product-Aware → Most Aware

**4. Gather VOC from multiple sources**
- Immerse in customer language across channels
- Look for patterns: recurring words, objections, desires

**5. Mine for the "Goldmine" phrases**
- Exact customer language that can be lifted verbatim into copy
- Phrases that capture frustration, aspiration, or relief
- Words that describe the transformation your product enables

---

## Applying VOC Data to Copy

### Mirror the Customer's Language
Don't translate customer language into marketing speak. Use their actual words. If customers say "I hate switching between tabs," your copy says "Stop switching between tabs."

### Identify and Address Objections
VOC reveals what's holding people back. Address these objections directly in copy — near pricing pages, signup forms, product features.

### Stage-Matched Messaging
Match your copy's language and depth to where the reader is in their awareness journey:
- Problem-aware readers need empathy and validation
- Solution-aware readers need education and comparison
- Most-aware readers need a compelling offer and urgency

---

## VOC in Practice: The Demand Gen / Lead Gen Funnel

**Demand gen copy (top-of-funnel):**
- Use VOC to create content that educates and resonates at scale
- Mirror the language of problem-aware prospects
- Distribution channels: Blog, podcast, social, influencer

**Lead gen copy (bottom-of-funnel):**
- Use VOC to craft lead magnet copy that speaks directly to specific pain points
- Mirror the language of product-aware or most-aware prospects
- Always include one clear CTA per landing page

---

## Key Copyhackers Rules for VOC Research

1. Start every project with research — no exceptions
2. Adopt a scientist's curiosity throughout
3. Look for the words that make your ideal customer say "that's exactly how I feel"
4. Quantity matters in subject lines: brainstorm 50+, #47 will be the winner
5. Unsolicited feedback > prompted feedback for genuine insight
6. Use VOC to optimize the OFFER, not just the copy words
