# How To Write Emails: The Conversion Copywriter's Approach

Source: https://copyhackers.com/2023/10/how-to-write-emails/

## Key Takeaways
- Mastering email copy means following a simple 3-part process
- Conversion copywriters use Voice of Customer (VOC) data and frameworks — never stare at the blank page
- Email is a highly effective revenue-generating machine

## The 3-Stage Conversion Copywriting Process for Email

### Stage 1: Research & Discovery

**The two critical questions before writing:**
1. What do you need to say?
2. How should you say it?

Both answered via Voice of Customer (VOC) data.

#### Voice of Customer (VOC) Data
VOC data = collective insights and feedback directly from customers. Their desires, pain points, and words spoken straight from the heart. It lets you mirror their language and create a deeper connection.

**How to gather VOC:**
- Surveys and interviews
- Social media conversations
- Product reviews
- Customer feedback across channels
- Unsolicited feedback (most genuine)

#### The "List, Offer, Copy" Framework
1. **List** — Who are you talking to? (subscribers, website visitors, specific segment)
2. **Offer** — What value proposition are you presenting? What's the goal of this email?
3. **Copy** — Armed with list + offer insights, craft the persuasive message

#### Optimize Your Offer
- Address objections that prevent conversion
- Example: for team software, add complimentary onboarding consultation to address "hassle of transition" objection
- Offer optimization ≠ just discounting — it's enhancing perceived value

#### Identify Reader's Stage of Awareness
- **Unaware** — no awareness of how your offer helps
- **Problem-Aware** — knows they have a problem
- **Solution-Aware** — actively seeking solutions
- **Product-Aware** — considering specific products/brands
- **Most Aware** — just needs a final nudge

Your email should move readers from their current stage to Most Aware.

#### Nail Your Lead + Pick a Copywriting Framework
- Brainstorm hooks that match reader's awareness stage
- Popular framework: **PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution)**
  - Problem: identify the reader's pain
  - Agitation: intensify to evoke response
  - Solution: introduce your offer as the answer
- Other frameworks: check copyhackers.com/2015/10/copywriting-formula/

### Stage 2: Draft, Format, and Edit

**Step-by-step:**
1. Write your CTA first (know where you're going)
2. Craft a captivating subject line — brainstorm 50+ options; use VOC language
3. Write the email body using your VOC-informed outline
4. Transition smoothly to the optimized offer
5. Format it (adjust margins to match inbox appearance)
6. **Edit in the Awesome** — remove "depletion of assets" language; ensure every line moves reader toward YES

**Subject line resources:**
- Email subject lines: The rules + the ways to win
- Writing email subject lines to improve open rates

### Stage 3: Validate & Experiment
- **A/B test** variables: subject line, opening hook, CTA
- **Measure metrics** beyond open rates — understand behavioral patterns
- **Continuously improve** — every campaign is data for the next one

## Core Principles
- Research first, always — VOC data is the compass
- Never sit down to write without a process
- Offer optimization often matters more than copy tweaks
- Every line must serve the conversion goal
