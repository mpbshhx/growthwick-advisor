## Website Copy: The Copyhackers Framework

### The Purpose of Website Copy
Every word on your website has one job: move the visitor to the next step. Whether that's reading the next section, clicking a CTA, or picking up the phone.

Website copy is not about sounding smart. It's about guiding visitors toward action.

---

## Homepage Copy Structure

### Above the Fold
The most valuable real estate on your website. Must answer three questions in seconds:
1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why should I care?

**The hero headline:** Your value proposition, stated clearly. Not clever. Clear.

### Below the Fold
Expand on the promise. Cover:
- How it works (the process)
- Social proof (testimonials, logos, user counts)
- Key benefits (not features — benefits)
- FAQ / Objection handling
- CTA (one per section, consistent throughout)

## The Joanna Wiebe Method: Voice of Customer Copy

**Don't write website copy from your own head. Mine it from your customers.**

The best website copy is often:
- A customer quote used as a headline
- The exact words someone used to describe their problem, used as an intro
- The phrases customers use when they recommend you to friends

### Process:
1. Collect VOC data (reviews, interviews, survey responses)
2. Identify the most emotionally charged language
3. Use that language verbatim in headlines, subheads, and CTAs
4. Test it against your own "clever" copy

VOC copy consistently outperforms copy written from the inside-out.

## About Page Copy
The about page is not about you. It's about why you're the right choice for them.

Structure:
1. Lead with the reader's problem or aspiration
2. Introduce your mission in terms of what you do FOR them
3. Build credibility (experience, credentials, social proof)
4. Human element — the people behind the product
5. CTA — direct them to the next step

## SaaS Website Copy Principles

### Features vs Benefits
Never lead with features. Lead with benefits.
- Feature: "Real-time collaboration"
- Benefit: "Everyone edits at once — no more emailing files back and forth"

### Clarity vs Cleverness
Clever copy that confuses converts less than clear copy that's "boring."
- Unclear: "The future of workflow optimization"
- Clear: "Manage your team's projects in one place"

### Short vs Long Copy
There is no universally correct length. Length should match:
- The complexity of the offer
- The price point
- The reader's stage of awareness
- The level of trust required

High-ticket / high-commitment = more copy needed.
Low-friction / low-commitment = less copy needed.

## Key Website Copy Rules (Copyhackers Principles)

1. **Write to one person** — not "our customers" but "you"
2. **Use active voice** — "Get results" not "Results are achieved"
3. **Lead with the reader's world** — their problem, their language, their aspiration
4. **Earn the scroll** — every section must give them a reason to keep reading
5. **Every CTA should state the benefit** — "Start my free trial" not "Submit"
6. **Remove friction words** — "just," "simply," "easy" often backfire
7. **Address the biggest objection** — find it via VOC, answer it on the page
