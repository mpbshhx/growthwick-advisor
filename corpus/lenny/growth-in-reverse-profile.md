# Lenny Rachitsky: The Man Who Built His Email List to Over 377K — Growth In Reverse
**Source:** https://growthinreverse.com/lenny/
**Type:** Business growth case study

## How Lenny Makes Money
- **Paid Subscriptions:** $15/mo or $150/yr, plus "I Can Expense It" tier at $300
  - 377K+ free subscribers, ~4-8% conversion to paid
  - Estimated ~$2M/year from newsletter (after Substack 10% + Stripe 3%)
- **Podcast Sponsors:** Estimated ~$2M/year (podcast outearns newsletter)
- **Job Board:** Premium listings, talent collective matching
- **Angel Investing:** Portfolio of companies he advises/invests in
- **Estimated total: $4M+ per year minimum**

## Growth Timeline

### Phase 1: Medium (2019)
- Left Airbnb, wrote "What Seven Years at Airbnb Taught Me" on Medium
- Got 29,000 claps, Brian Chesky shared with entire Airbnb
- Was planning to build a startup, not a newsletter
- Andrew Chen told him: "It's rare to find something you love doing, that the market also loves"
- Moved readers to Substack newsletter → first ~100 subscribers

### Phase 2: Guest Posting (2019)
- Guest post on Andrew Chen's site (a16z partner)
- Post on First Round Review
- Both guest posts → reached 1,000 subscribers

### Phase 3: Deep Research (Late 2019)
- Published massive marketplace research: interviewed 25 people from 17 companies (Uber, Airbnb, DoorDash, Thumbtack, etc.)
- 4-part series nearly doubled his ~2,800 subscriber base
- This established him as someone who "would put in the work"

### Phase 4: First 1,000 Users Research
- Spent a month interviewing founders about their first 1,000 users
- Became his most popular post of all time

### Phase 5: Substack Recommendations (April 2022)
- Substack launched cross-promotion recommendations feature
- Lenny's growth curve went exponential
- Other writers recommending his newsletter on signup

### Phase 6: Twitter Flywheel
- Sustainable audience growth converting to subscribers
- Not a "thread boi" but consistent presence

## Key Growth Levers
1. Getting in front of other audiences (Medium, guest posts)
2. Deep research on the industry (quality that justified sharing)
3. Substack Recommendations (platform leverage)
4. Twitter Flywheel (consistent conversion)
