# What Seven Years at Airbnb Taught Me About Building a Business — Lenny Rachitsky
**Source:** https://marker.medium.com/what-seven-years-at-airbnb-taught-me-about-building-a-company-e1d035d49c56
**Published:** April 2, 2019
**Type:** Personal essay / lessons learned

## Context
Lenny joined Airbnb in 2012 after Airbnb acquired his startup (Localmind). He started as an engineer, then became one of the first members of the PM team. When he joined there were "a couple dozen engineers, a few designers, and two very cute dogs." Over seven years, the company scaled to thousands of global employees and over $30B in value.

## Key Lessons

### 1. Create Strong Culture and Values
- Co-founder Joe Gebbia's direction to a designer redesigning the homepage: "Build something the internet has never seen before"
- This mindset — setting wildly ambitious bars — was one of the key ingredients in Airbnb's historic growth
- Culture isn't just posters on walls; it's how decisions get made

### 2. Stay Laser-Focused on Problems
- Focus on the problem, not the solution
- Understand the problem deeply before building anything

### 3. Set Wildly Ambitious Goals
- The ambitious goal-setting culture pushed everyone to think bigger
- High bars for quality and innovation

## Significance
This was Lenny's first viral piece after leaving Airbnb. The CEO Brian Chesky shared it with the entire company. It directly led to the creation of Lenny's Newsletter — a friend (Andrew Chen) told him "it's rare to find something you love doing, that the market also loves" and convinced him to keep writing instead of starting another startup.
