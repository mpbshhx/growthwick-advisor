# How the Biggest Consumer Apps Got Their First 1,000 Users — Lenny Rachitsky
**Source:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/consumer-business-find-first-users
**Type:** Research-based framework (100+ companies studied)

## The Seven Reliable Ways to Find First 1,000 Users

1. **Reach out to friends and colleagues** (~20% of startups)
   - Easiest and fastest
   - Examples: Yelp (PayPal network), Lyft (personal invites)
   - Best for: Products most people find useful

2. **Reach out to targeted strangers**
   - Cold DM (Cameo), cold email (YouTube), phone (Airbnb), door-to-door (DoorDash)
   - Best for: Marketplaces and platforms

3. **Go where target audience hangs out** (online or offline)
   - Online: Dropbox/HackerNews, Discord/Reddit
   - Offline: Etsy/craft fairs, Snapchat/malls, Tinder/college campuses
   - Morning Brew/business schools, Lyft/startup offices

4. **Enlist influencers** (paid or organic)
   - Twitter used early influencer adoption
   - Works best when your product is sharable

5. **Get press**
   - Udemy: PR as primary driver
   - Best if you have a remarkable story

6. **Create viral content**
   - Blog post (Superhuman), Video (Duolingo), One-off website (Calm)

7. **Get physical placement**
   - Flyers/handouts (DoorDash), Stickers (Grubhub), Signs (Tinder)

## Three Common Archetypes

### Typical Consumer Startup Path
1. Friends and colleagues → 2. Where audience hangs out → 3. Influencers

### Marketplace/Platform Path
1. Targeted strangers → 2. Physical placement → 3. Supply drives demand

### Remarkable Story Path
1. Press → 2. Viral content

## Key Insight
"Almost every startup finds the vast majority of their early growth from just one or two channels. Focus. Don't order everything on the menu."
