# Lenny Rachitsky's Product Strategy Essentials
**Source:** https://www.theproductfolks.com/product-management-blog/lenny-rachitskys-product-strategy-essentials
**Type:** Framework synthesis

## Core Philosophy
- Truly understand users' needs before building anything
- Eschew assumptions in favor of research — talking to customers, analyzing data and market trends
- "Build the smallest possible product that will enable user feedback, and improve iteratively based on what you learn"

## Market Analysis Framework

### 1. Assess Market Size and Trends
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) estimation
- Market growth trends — prioritize growing markets
- Historic growth rates and future projections

### 2. Segment Potential Customers
- Demographic, Geographic, Behavioral, Psychographic
- Get very specific in segmentation

### 3. Competitive Landscape and Opportunity Gaps
- Chart existing solutions by features, pricing, target segments
- Look for gaps where competitors fail to satisfy customer needs
- Iteratively analyze and refine hypotheses

## Understanding Customer Needs

### Qualitative Methods
- User interviews with open-ended questions
- Observation of actual product usage and friction points
- Ethnographic research — embed with target users

### Quantitative Methods
- DAU/MAU, retention cohorts, funnel drop-off analysis
- A/B testing features with target segments
- Support tickets, NPS surveys, app reviews for recurring pain points

### User Personas
- Include goals, behaviors, pain points, product needs
- Map to target customer segments
- Reference in strategy discussions to ground decisions in reality

## Key Principle
Strategy should adapt based on evidence from real users — not hypotheticals or gut feelings. Focus on metrics that reveal whether you're actually solving customer needs, not vanity metrics like signups and engagement.
