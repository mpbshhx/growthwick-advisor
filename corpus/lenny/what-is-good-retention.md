# What Is Good Retention — Lenny Rachitsky & Casey Winters
**Source:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-is-good-retention-issue-29
**Type:** Research-backed benchmark framework
**Co-author:** Casey Winters (ex-Pinterest, Grubhub, Eventbrite CPO)

## Key Insight
"Great retention is the scalable way to grow a product. It's the best indicator of product-market fit, it is the most important factor in a user's lifetime value, and high retention drives all of the best acquisition strategies." — Casey Winters

## User Retention Benchmarks (at 6 months)

### Consumer Social (e.g., Snapchat, Twitter, Instagram)
- GOOD: ~25%
- GREAT: ~45%
- Public comps: Facebook 60-70%, Instagram 50-60%, Snapchat 33% (3-mo), Twitter 31% (3-mo)

### Consumer Transactional (e.g., Airbnb, Lyft, TurboTax)
- GOOD: ~30%
- GREAT: ~50%
- Public comps: TurboTax 77% (12-mo), Lyft 22% (12-mo)

### Consumer SaaS (e.g., Netflix, Spotify, Hulu)
- GOOD: ~40%
- GREAT: ~70%
- Public comps: Amazon Prime 93%, Dropbox ~80%, Spotify 72%, Netflix 66%, Hulu 53%

### SMB/Mid-Market SaaS (e.g., Asana, Slack, Atlassian)
- GOOD: ~60%
- GREAT: ~80%
- Public comps: Atlassian 98%, Slack 90-95%, QuickBooks 79%

### Enterprise SaaS (e.g., Salesforce, Workday)
- GOOD: ~70%
- GREAT: ~90%

## Net Revenue Retention Benchmarks (at 12 months)

### Consumer SaaS
- GOOD: ~55%, GREAT: ~80%

### Bottom-up SaaS
- GOOD: ~100%, GREAT: ~120%

### Land-and-Expand VSB SaaS
- GOOD: ~80%, GREAT: ~100%

### Land-and-Expand SMB/Mid-Market SaaS
- GOOD: ~90%, GREAT: ~110%

### Enterprise SaaS
- GOOD: ~110%, GREAT: ~130%

## When Lower Retention Is Acceptable
1. You're just starting out
2. You have low CAC and marginal costs
3. You're not building a venture-scale business
