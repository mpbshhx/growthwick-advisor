# How to Know If You've Got Product-Market Fit — Lenny Rachitsky

**Type:** Curated framework with expert definitions

## Pre-Product PMF Signals (Before Launch)

### Qualitative Signals
- "Do someone's pupils dilate when they use your stuff?" — Steve Blank
- "Very strong customer feedback, even from a small group... literal love letters from customers." — Elad Gil
- "Are people willing to pay you? Try to get them to pay now — send an invoice for early access." — Lenny

### Quantitative Signals
- **Retention curve flattens:** Plot % active users over time for cohorts. If it flattens, you have PMF for some audience. — Brian Balfour
- **Cohort retention:** Look at a group that tried your product in one month, see how many use it 12 months later. Does it flatten? — Casey Winters
- **Sean Ellis Test:** Survey users "How would you feel if you could no longer use this?" If >40% say "very disappointed," you have PMF — Sean Ellis/Rahul Vohra

## Post-Product PMF Signals

### For Enterprise
- "Pull the trial. If the customer doesn't scream, you don't have PMF. If they aren't desperate, you don't have PMF." — Doug Leone/Andy Rachleff
- **Sales yield >1.0:** Contribution margin of sales team / total cost to field team > 1.0 — Andy Rachleff
- **SaaS: Major brands finding and using you organically and paying** — Elad Gil

### For Consumer
- "Exponential organic growth driven by word of mouth" — Andy Rachleff
- "Explosive and destructive usage" — Michael Seibel
- "Do any users love the product so much they spontaneously tell others?" — Sam Altman
- "Ideally >50% of new accounts from direct or organic traffic" — Merci Victoria Grace

### Financial Measures
- **Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR** — Lower is better. Market pulling product out vs. pushing product onto market. — David Sacks
- "PMF when you can repeatably acquire customers for less than what they're worth" — Elizabeth Yin
- "Value of each user > cost of bringing them in" — Nikhyl Singhal
- "Retention that creates enough $ or content/virality to drive sustainable acquisition" — Casey Winters

## The Feel of PMF
"You can always feel product/market fit when it's happening. Customers are buying as fast as you can make it. Usage growing as fast as you can add servers. Money piling up in your checking account." — Marc Andreessen

"Pushing a boulder = don't have PMF. Chasing a boulder = have PMF." — Attributed saying
