# Demand: Where Most B2B Marketing Stalls

By Evan Hughes | June 30, 2025

## The Core Insight

"If brand builds the reputation, demand captures the moment."

But too often, B2B teams jump straight to demand without laying the groundwork. That's why the ad spend feels inefficient. That's why inbound leads fall flat. That's why sales is constantly asking for "better leads."

A demand engine isn't just lead gen. It's the system that turns interest into pipeline.

## What Demand Is Really Responsible For

Demand isn't just filling the funnel. It's about helping the right buyers take the next step, when they're ready, on their terms:

- Guiding prospects from awareness into evaluation
- Making sure high-intent paths are clear and frictionless
- Creating urgency without pressure
- Getting your team in front of the right accounts at the right time

"If your motion is still 'run ads, get leads, pass to sales' — that's not demand gen. That's marketing as a task rabbit."

## Why Demand Often Breaks Down

**1. No clear definition of "high intent"**
If every lead counts the same in your CRM, you're not segmenting correctly. Buyers who ask to talk to sales need a different path than someone downloading a PDF.

**2. You're over-rotated on paid**
You can't just pour more budget into LinkedIn and expect results. If brand hasn't been built, and landing pages don't convert, you're setting money on fire.

**3. Your metrics don't reflect revenue**
If your top-line goals are leads or MQLs, you're optimizing for volume, not quality. Demand needs to be accountable to pipeline, not just performance marketing metrics.

## What a Good Demand Program Looks Like

- **Strong offer clarity:** Buyers know exactly what the next step is, and why it matters
- **Fast sales speed:** Sales follows up within hours. No lag. No handoff confusion.
- **Targeted engagement:** Spending budget and effort on the right accounts, not just whoever clicks
- **Aligned messaging:** Demand creative matches what sales is saying on calls
- **High conversion on direct and organic:** Your website isn't just traffic — it's action

## The Signals That Your Demand Engine Is Working

- Demo requests converting to qualified opps at 30%+
- Pipeline velocity going up (faster cycles, higher win rates, better-fit deals)
- High intent traffic increasing without needing to inflate spend
- Outbound working better because prospects already understand the value
- Sales actually wants the leads

## How to Measure Demand Without Chasing MQLs

Focus on what actually matters:
- **Cost per high-intent demo** — efficiency of capturing in-market demand
- **SQO rate (demo → opp)** — lead quality and qualification rigor
- **Pipeline velocity** — how fast and effectively deals move
- **Attribution mix** — where actual revenue comes from, not just where it was clicked
- **Conversion by source** — which channels drive real pipeline

## Where to Look First If Demand Isn't Driving Pipeline

- **Your website:** Is it clear? Can someone figure out what you do in under 10 seconds?
- **Your offer:** Is the CTA actually valuable — or just "book a demo"?
- **Your budget:** Are you overspending on the wrong segment or channels?
- **Your CRM definitions:** Do marketing and sales agree on what counts as qualified?
- **Your speed to lead:** Do high-intent leads get a response immediately?

"Every missed conversion point is a demand leak. Patch those first before spending more."

## The Bottom Line

"Demand is what makes your marketing real. It's what connects the narrative to the pipeline. It's where your buyer finally steps forward and says, 'I'm ready.' But they only do that if you've made it easy."
