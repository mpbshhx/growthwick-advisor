# The Attribution Mirage

By Megan Bowen & David Kirkdorffer | October 15, 2024

## What Is the Attribution Mirage?

A phenomenon that leads modern B2B marketers astray — impacting staffing, budgeting, and everything marketing teams work on.

The attribution mirage occurs when a person, organization, or system attributes a success or failure to the wrong cause. This happens when the true cause is complex or difficult to determine, and people instead attribute it to simpler or more easily identifiable factors.

## Three Eras of B2B Buying

**Analog Era:**
- Few sources of product information; buyers go to trade shows, consult analysts
- Sellers mostly control the flow of information
- Relatively few choices in any product category

**Website Era:**
- Buyers can complete entire evaluation and purchase process without a sales rep
- Purchase process involves wider set of stakeholders
- Increasing number of choices in any product category

**Dark Social Era:**
- Hyper-connected B2B peers use digital channels (social, communities, word-of-mouth) to share information, opinions, education, and advocacy
- Buyers can do their own research, tapping trusted peers while remaining anonymous until ready for sales
- Buyer is often end-user; user-led decisions control the buying experience
- Overwhelming number of choices

## How the Attribution Mirage Happens

**Illustrative scenario — a CMO investigating technology:**
1. She asks trusted colleagues in a Slack community for recommendations
2. They suggest 3 solutions she's never heard of
3. She Googles those solution names (Companies A, B, C)
4. She sees a Google Ad for Company D and clicks it
5. She books demos for all four

**Software attribution result:** 2 companies = "SEO," 2 companies = "Paid Ads"
**Self-reported reality:** For A, B, and C — the real source was the Slack community thread

Only Company D is correctly attributed. The other 3 are the mirage.

## The Data: A 12-Month Research Study

Refine Labs ran a 12-month test capturing both software-based AND self-reported attribution on their "Book a Strategy Call" form.

**Sample: 620 conversions**

**For High-Intent Leads:**
- Software attribution said: 78% from web searches
- Self-reported said: only 12% from web searches
- Self-reported said: **85% from Dark Social** (social media, podcast, word of mouth, community)
- Podcast and community: NOT measured at all by software

**For Closed/Won Revenue:**
- Self-reported: **98% from Dark Social**
- Software attribution: 79% from web search — but customers said only 3%
- **'Podcast' has the highest conversion rate of any source to Closed/Won**

## The Fix: Hybrid Attribution

Attribution should be measured on two levels:
- **Capture Demand Attribution** = software-based (measures digital interactions)
- **Create Demand Attribution** = self-reported (measures where awareness actually started)

Combined = **Hybrid Attribution**

**How to capture self-reported attribution:**
Add a mandatory open text-field question — no drop-downs, no suggestions — to key intake forms: "How did you hear about us?" Let prospects answer in their own words.

Examples of real answers:
- "From the XYZ podcast"
- "I've been following one of your employee's posts on LinkedIn"
- "I was referred to your company by someone in a Slack channel"
- "I know one of your customers. He uses you and has nothing but good things to say"

## Why This Matters

Software-based attribution dramatically:
- **Over-reports** organic search and direct links
- **Under-reports** social media sources
- **Completely misses:** LinkedIn/Facebook communities, YouTube, Podcasts, Slack communities, internal company comms, word of mouth, text messages, DMs, events

"If we relied only on software-based attribution, it would look like our social media and podcast strategy were failing. But by capturing self-reported attribution, we see a very different story."

## Key Takeaway

"Dark social sources yield the most high-quality Opportunities that Close/Won — and software-based attribution is blind to this."

The solution isn't to abandon software attribution. It's to add self-reported attribution alongside it for a complete picture of how demand is created vs. captured.
