# The Attribution Mirage

## What Is It?

The attribution mirage is a phenomenon that leads modern B2B marketers astray. It impacts every aspect of B2B marketing — from staffing to budgeting, and everything marketing teams work on.

An attribution mirage occurs when a person, organization, or system attributes a success or failure to the wrong cause. This happens when the true cause is complex or difficult to determine, and people instead attribute it to simpler or more easily identifiable factors.

## Three Eras of B2B Buying

**Analog Era:**
- Few sources of product information
- Buyers go to trade shows, consult with analysts
- Sellers mostly control the flow of information
- Relatively few choices in any product category

**Website Era:**
- Lots of sources of 'always on' information
- Buyers can complete entire evaluation without a sales rep
- Purchase process involves a wider set of stakeholders
- Increasing choices in any product category

**Dark Social Era:**
- Overwhelming number of sources of information
- Hyper-connected B2B peers use digital channels (social networks, communities, word-of-mouth) to share information and opinions
- Buyers can remain anonymous until ready to start a sales conversation
- Buyer is often the end-user; emotions and peer influences play bigger roles

## How The Mirage Happens

Real scenario: A CMO investigating technology asks trusted colleagues in a Slack community. They suggest three solutions. She Googles them. Software attribution credits: SEO and Paid Ads. Reality: Slack community was the true source.

Software attribution:
- Dramatically over-reports organic search and direct links
- Significantly under-reports social media sources
- Doesn't track dark social: LinkedIn, Facebook Groups, Slack communities, podcasts, DMs, meetups

## Research Data: 12-Month Study (620 Conversions)

**Software says:**
- 78% of conversions sourced from web searches

**Customers actually report:**
- 85% of conversions sourced from Dark Social (social media, podcast, word of mouth, community)
- Web searches only 12% of the time

**For Closed/Won deals:**
- 98% heard through Dark Social
- Podcast = highest percentage contribution and conversion rate
- Software said 79% came from web search; customers reported only 3%

**Key finding:** Podcast listeners convert to revenue at the highest rate of any channel — and software attribution is completely blind to this.

## The Fix: Hybrid Attribution

Attribution should be measured on two levels:

1. **Capture Demand Attribution** (software-based) — where people interact with your digital presence
2. **Create Demand Attribution** (self-reported) — where people actually first heard about you

Self-reported attribution: Add mandatory open text-field ("How did you hear about us?") to key intake forms. No drop-downs. No suggestions. Let them answer in their own words.

## Why The Problem Is Getting Worse

B2B buyers increasingly learn through peers, social networks, communities, and podcasts. The more this happens, the more software-based attribution fails to capture the full picture.

Buyers are overwhelmed by:
- So many solution categories
- So many vendors in each category
- So much well-produced content making similar claims

They use social connections to speed through Awareness and Interest stages — which software can't see.

## Conclusion

Software attribution = good at measuring where people interact with your digital footprint.

Self-reported attribution = good at measuring where people actually first learn about solutions.

Both are needed. Neither alone gives you the full picture.
