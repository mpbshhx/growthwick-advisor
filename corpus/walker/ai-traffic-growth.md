# How We Grew AI-Referred Traffic 5–6× in 9 Months

By Evan Hughes | March 3, 2026

## The Headlines

- **5–6× peak AI session growth** (Dec 2025 vs. Jan 2025)
- **4–5× sustained lift** (still running Feb 2026)
- **~80% ChatGPT share** throughout
- **$0 paid** to acquire — zero paid media involved

## The Core Thesis

"We made a deliberate decision to change how we write — specifically, to write the way language models retrieve, not the way search engines rank."

**LLMs don't rank pages. They decide what to cite. That's a different optimization target.**

## The Diagnosis (Q1 2025)

Why we weren't getting cited:
- Low semantic clarity — scattered across many topics, not deeply owning any
- Pages written for narrative engagement rather than answer extraction
- No frameworks, models, or structured breakdowns LLMs could pull from cleanly
- Optimized for search (understood) not for AI referral (growing fast)

## The Pivot (Q2 2025 — April Inflection)

### What Actually Changed

**1. Rebuilt content around clear topic ownership**
Instead of publishing broadly on B2B marketing, picked specific themes and went deep: Brand vs. Demand vs. Expand as distinct pillars, GTM maturity models, revenue alignment frameworks. LLMs respond to structured topic authority — not keyword density.

**2. Rewrote openings for answer extraction**
Every page got a new first 100 words. Instead of narrative warmups, opened with a direct definition: what this topic is, why it matters, what the page covers. LLMs extract clean sections — they don't read narrative introductions the way humans do.

**3. Rebuilt H-tag hierarchies as standalone questions**
Every H2 was rewritten to function as a question a buyer might ask. Test: can you read just this section and get a complete answer without surrounding context? This single change likely drove more LLM citations than anything else in Q2.

**4. Replaced narrative prose with frameworks**
Any concept that could be expressed as a model, stage-by-stage process, or framework was. LLMs cite frameworks constantly — they're extractable, attributable, and memorable. Abstract opinion pieces get ignored. Structured frameworks get cited.

## Building Depth (Q3 2025)

Published "cornerstone content" — not blog posts, but reference documents:
- Brand Deep Dive (3,000+ words) → primarily cited by ChatGPT, Gemini
- Demand Deep Dive (3,000+ words) → ChatGPT, Claude
- GTM Maturity Model → all LLMs (named frameworks get cited)
- Revenue Alignment Frameworks → ChatGPT, Claude
- AI for Marketers primer → all LLMs (definitional content performs consistently)

## Compound Growth (Q4 2025)

The cornerstone content from Q2 and Q3 had been indexed, cited, and reinforced. New content published in Q4 picked up traction faster. Each new piece benefited from established authority in topic clusters.

## Why This Worked

**Topic ownership compounds.** The biggest driver was the cluster effect. Once 3-4 deeply structured pages on Brand, Demand, and Expand existed, new content in those areas picked up traction faster. LLMs have a model of what topics different sources are authoritative on.

**Structure is a feature.** The highest-leverage change: treating H-tags as answer containers rather than navigational labels. Every H2 should stand alone as a question, with its section as the answer.

**Depth beats volume.** A 2,500-word deep dive consistently outperforms five 500-word takes on the same topic for LLM citation purposes.

**Technical structure multiplies content quality.** Clean URLs, proper H-tag hierarchy, logical internal linking — these determine whether LLMs can correctly attribute content to your domain.

## Source Breakdown (Feb 2026)
- ChatGPT: ~80%
- Perplexity: ~12%
- Gemini: ~6%
- Claude + Copilot: ~2% (emerging, growing)

## The Takeaway

"December was a peak. February is the new floor. That's the difference between a spike and infrastructure."

If AI referral traffic is still flat for your B2B site, the gap is almost certainly structural — not creative. The question is whether an LLM can find a clear answer in your content and attribute it to you with confidence.
