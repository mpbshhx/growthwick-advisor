# Brand, Demand, Expand: A Better Way to Structure B2B Marketing

By Evan Hughes | May 28, 2025

## The Core Insight

Marketing didn't break. We just stopped using it the right way. Most B2B teams are stuck chasing pipeline without stepping back to ask: are we building something sustainable? Or are we just filling gaps month to month?

The shift needed: from a lead-chasing mindset to a strategy that compounds over time.

**Brand. Demand. Expand.** These three motions already exist inside your company. You just haven't aligned around them yet.

## Why This Matters Now

The SaaS buyer has changed. They don't want to be prospected. They want to explore, evaluate, and engage on their own terms. That journey isn't linear. And it doesn't belong to just one team.

Brand, Demand, Expand isn't a new playbook. It's a way to organize your go-to-market efforts around what actually drives revenue and prevents churn.

## The Three Motions

### Brand: Gets You in the Conversation Before Buyers Raise Their Hand

"If people don't know you, they won't buy from you."

Brand is how you show up when no one's actively looking for you yet. It's trust, relevance, clarity, consistency. It's what gets you remembered when they're ready to evaluate.

**Key stat:** In a market where 70% of research happens before sales ever gets a meeting, brand matters more than most teams give it credit for.

It's not just logos and impressions. It's what creates preference.

### Demand: Turns Interest Into Pipeline

This is where most B2B marketers focus: paid search, content downloads, website CTAs, retargeting, demo requests.

**The catch:** Demand can't carry the whole team. High-performing demand programs only work when your brand has already done the heavy lifting.

"If you're running demand in a vacuum, you're playing a short game."

### Expand: What Makes Growth Efficient

Most teams stop at the deal. That's the mistake.

Expand is everything that happens after the contract is signed. It's how you activate the customer, build advocates, unlock upsell, and increase retention.

"It's not just CS. It's a company-wide commitment to delivering on what marketing promised."

If you're not investing in Expand, you're leaking revenue every quarter — and it shows up in CAC, LTV, and NRR.

## The Framework Summary

"Brand gets you known. Demand gets you chosen. Expand makes you profitable."

And every motion needs:
- An owner
- A budget
- A set of metrics tied to revenue

When marketing, sales, product, and CS align around these motions, you don't just hit the number. You actually build momentum.

## Quick Diagnostic

If most of your programs fall under Demand, you're not alone. But it also means you're not building long-term leverage. You don't need to force equal weight, but you do need intent behind what you're doing and why.

Questions to ask:
- Do we have a strategy for each motion?
- Or are we hoping good creative and more ads will save the quarter?
