# Brand, Demand, Expand: A Better Way to Structure B2B Marketing

## The Core Framework

Marketing didn't break. We just stopped using it the right way.

Most B2B teams are stuck chasing pipeline without stepping back to ask the bigger question: are we building something sustainable? Or are we just filling gaps month to month?

**Brand. Demand. Expand.** — Three motions that already exist inside your company. You just haven't aligned around them yet.

## Why This Matters Now

The SaaS buyer has changed. They don't want to be prospected. They want to explore, evaluate, and engage on their own terms.

That journey isn't linear. And it definitely doesn't belong to just one team.

In a market where **70% of research happens before sales ever gets a meeting**, brand matters more than most teams give it credit for.

## The Three Motions

### Brand: Gets You In the Conversation Before Buyers Raise Their Hand
- Trust, relevance, clarity, consistency
- What creates preference before buyers are ready to evaluate
- Not just logos and impressions — it's what gets you remembered

### Demand: Turns Interest Into Pipeline
- Paid search, content downloads, website CTAs, retargeting, demo requests
- Demand programs only work when brand has already done the heavy lifting
- Running demand in a vacuum = playing a short game

### Expand: Makes Growth Efficient
- Everything that happens after the contract is signed
- Activates customers, builds advocates, unlocks upsell, increases retention
- Not just CS — a company-wide commitment to delivering on what marketing promised
- If you're not investing in expand, you're leaking revenue every quarter (shows up in CAC, LTV, NRR)

## Quick Reference Mapping

"If most of your programs fall under demand, you're not alone. But it also means you're not building long-term leverage."

You don't need equal weight — but you need intent behind what you're doing and why.

## Summary

**Brand** gets you known.  
**Demand** gets you chosen.  
**Expand** makes you profitable.

Every motion needs an owner, a budget, a set of metrics tied to revenue.

When marketing, sales, product, and CS align around these motions, you don't just hit the number. You build momentum.
