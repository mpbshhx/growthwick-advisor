# How to Build Your Career in the Age of AI. You're Being Lied To — Kieran Flanagan

*Published June 5, 2026*

## The Core Lie

Every podcast, tech billionaire, and AI influencer is saying the same thing about careers in the AI era: Work harder. Have agency. Adapt or die.

Almost all of it is being written by people who've already made their money, had great careers, and aren't under the same pressures as people still trying to build one.

## Three Worker Categories

**The Coasters** — clock in, clock out, do the basics. Work is a paycheck. AI is something they've heard about, not used or cared about. Going to be hard to compete with LLM models.

**The Pros** — care about their craft. Do excellent work between 9 and 5. Close the laptop for dinner. Want a good career and a good life, a balance. Should be able to have a full and fulfilling career.

**The Possessed** — work is identity. No line between work and personal life. Trade hobbies, relationships, and health for career wins.

There's nothing wrong with any of these categories. There should be optionality for how people want to approach work vs. life.

**The problem:** Every piece of 2026 AI career advice is from people in the Possessed category, for an audience the Possessed assume should become them.

## The Macro vs. Micro Reality

**The easy macro story:** AI gets better. Workers adapt. New jobs replace old ones. People with high agency adapt. Reid Hoffman: "AI gives you superpowers for the entire world of information, navigation, decision-making."

**The brutal micro story:**
- DHR Global's 2026 Workforce Trends Report: burnout sitting at **83%**
- Employee engagement collapsed from **88% to 64% in a single year**
- Nearly half of workers cited overwhelming workloads as the top driver of burnout
- 40% pointed to long hours
- Only **34%** said their employer clearly communicated how AI would affect their role
- BCG-Harvard study (March 2026, 1,488 US workers): coined "**AI brain fry**" — mental fatigue from excessive use or oversight of AI tools beyond one's cognitive capacity. Workers described foggy, buzzing feeling. Mental hangover. Headaches. Slower decision-making.

**The hidden cost:** Making the AI transition predominantly happens at night and on weekends. There's a period in your AI journey where your productivity gets WORSE as you rebuild how you work around AI.

## The Advice Industry's Blind Spot

The advice goes: read the papers, build the workflows, master the tools. Become irreplaceable. Be AI-native. Have agency. Simple.

It's 100% correct. But it obscures the trade-offs.

**The Possessed pattern isn't an easy life.** You trade off time with family, relationships, health, hobbies. I speak from personal experience. My life bears the scars of it. It's an investment. It will compound. But it's a real sacrifice.

The advice class doesn't tell you a trade is happening. And in their case, if the trade fails, the outcomes for them aren't that destructive. They've already won.

## What to Actually Do: 5 Things for Pros

### 1. Thirty minutes a day
Pick one workflow at a time. Most people pulling long hours around AI are doing too much and applying none of it. Real progress shows up in learning, application, and seeing actual results. Do one thing well vs. twenty things poorly.

### 2. Build a moat, not a stack
Don't chase every new tool. Don't watch every new YouTube video. Learn one tool, go deep. Build one defensible workflow that drives a real, meaningful outcome. **AI rewards depth of knowledge vs. shallow breadth.** Structure all AI learning around this one principle.

### 3. Choose learning, not just titles
The biggest variable in your career right now isn't your title. It's whether your current role allows you to integrate AI into your day job, or you have to keep it as a side quest.

There are periods in life when you optimize for the career ladder. Now is the time to optimize for learning: Am I in a role that will help me cross the 'AI chasm'?

### 4. Mute some of the 'grind' accounts
There's merit in following accounts that promote the 'AI grind.' But they can be overwhelming. Pick a couple. Tune out the rest.

### 5. Protect the off switch
Don't. Burn. Out.

## The Actual Point

Pro vs. Possessed is a choice. There are times in your life when you'll alternate between them both.

**Choosing balance isn't settling.** It's not a weakness. It's not a defensive crutch against AI. It's a legitimate way to build a life — a career and life, in that order — that most people across history have aspired to.

Choosing balance should be a viable option. It's not a weakness. It doesn't mean you should be steamrolled by the AI wave.

The damage isn't that some people choose Possessed. It's the advice industry pretending Pros are a casualty class who need to change their ways, instead of a category of people making a deliberate choice where you can have a real, serious career.

**You can have a real, serious career as a Pro. Everybody deserves a career to be proud of, in a way that can work for the life they want to build.**
