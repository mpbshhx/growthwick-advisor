# Inimitable Product is the New "Make Great Content"

By Rand Fishkin | 2026

## The Core Argument

For 25 years, Google told websites to "just make great content," and they'd sort out the rest. That advice was incomplete, but sorta true. If you made 10X Content in 1997, 2007, or 2017, it often worked.

But no more.

## What Google Is Doing

Google's plan is elegant, simple, and extractive:
- Crawl and index the web's content
- Use mafia-like power to force a prisoner's dilemma (if you don't let them index your stuff, they take it from someone else and reward them with the scraps of traffic)
- Use that content to train their models/power their answers
- Make your work a redundant commodity
- Only send clicks when businesses pay for ads OR offer something Google can't provide directly in their AI-powered answer interface

Result: Content becomes commoditized. The entire incentive structure for creating great content on the web breaks.

## The Two Solutions

### Solution 1 (Long-term, Unlikely)
Collective political action — voting for politicians who'd enforce antitrust, break up monopolies, make self-preferencing illegal. Cynically unlikely in today's political environment.

### Solution 2 (Short-term, Actionable): Build Inimitable Products

AI-powered search cannot:
- Invent ultrasonic chef's knives
- Tailor a made-to-measure suit with personality
- Thoughtfully curate gift boxes from local makers
- Prepare the meal of your life
- Source WWI-era Armagnac to serve your 98-year-old grandfather something older than him
- Mow your lawn, fix your roof, service your printer, manage your 401k

**Inimitable products = your defense.**

To build them:
1. Uncover the overlap between what you're passionately capable of creating, what other solutions fail to offer, and what people desperately want
2. Create that thing in a way designed for Zero Click Marketing — showable via short-form video, amplifiable with a few images, easily described by a rabid fan, enticing in a brief AI-answer summary
3. Get word out through channels where your audience pays attention: social, PR, advertising, search/AI, and word-of-mouth (which may return to its 20th-century MVP status)

## Is Content Marketing Dead?

Not quite dead, but "make great content" is no longer a viable strategy.

**Why Rand still publishes:**
- To influence search engines and AI tools (who still need sources to train from)
- To have a home base for an idea — something that can be cited, quoted, screenshotted, and referred back to
- To distribute via email — one of the last owned channels
- To exercise longer-form thinking that illustrates capability to the right people

**The cold math:** A post might get <5,000 visits, <500 from search, and <100 people who read every word. But if they're the right ones, and the piece creates external influence beyond that, it may still be worthwhile.

## The New Framework

- **Old:** Make great content → earn traffic → build business
- **New:** Build inimitable product → market through channels where audience pays attention → measure influence and branded search volume, not traffic

Content as product is dying. The New York Times is just a games platform with a vestigial news appendage. Bloomberg makes money on terminals and uses profits to fund content that markets the inimitable product.

**Inimitable content** is not defensible. **Inimitable products** are.

**The universal advice:** Ignore traffic. Make inimitable products. Shift priorities away from great content on your own site toward great marketing on the platforms where your audience pays attention.

Influence is the new traffic. Impressions and engagement are the new top of funnel. Branded search volume is the new middle. Sales remains the true measure of success.
