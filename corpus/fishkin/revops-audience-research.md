# Audience Research Brief: How We'd Market to Mid-Market RevOps Leaders

By SparkToro Team | 2026

## The Setup

This is the first issue of the Audience Research Brief series — running SparkToro reports on specific audiences to show what's unique about them and how we'd market to them.

**This week's audience:** Mid-market RevOps leaders at B2B SaaS companies.

A typical campaign might start with LinkedIn ABM, a Pavilion sponsorship, and a few blog posts around revenue operations. But that's guessing. Here's what the data actually shows.

## Insight #1: LinkedIn is Obvious. The Better Question is Where They Learn.

80.3% of this audience uses LinkedIn monthly, over-indexing by 15%. Yes, LinkedIn belongs in the plan.

**But the more interesting signal: Slack**
- 33.2% use Slack monthly, over-indexing by 201%
- This signals they work in fast-moving, cross-functional environments where internal conversations shape decisions

**The strategic implication:** You can't do demand gen in their Slack, but you can create things worth bringing into those internal conversations.

A useful RevOps asset is not just something that gets clicked. It's something a VP of Revenue can drop into Slack: "This is exactly what I've been trying to explain" or "Can we talk about this in our team meeting on Monday?"

That asset could be:
- A benchmark report
- A teardown of a common RevOps mistake
- A calculator or diagnostic
- A spicy-but-useful point of view
- A workflow template
- A comparison guide that makes an internal debate easier

**Also notable: Substack**
- 33.0% use Substack monthly, over-indexing by 44%
- Newsletter sponsorships, guest essays, creator partnerships, and earned mentions may be worth testing

**The real question:** Where does this audience find ideas they trust enough to repeat internally?

## Insight #2: This Audience Is on Claude. A Lot.

Roughly 1 in 3 people in this audience uses Claude, over-indexing by 438.4% compared to the US average.

This audience also over-indexes on ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Perplexity.

**This buyer is already asking AI tools questions before they ever get to your website.**

Top AI prompt topics for this audience:
- Best practices for RevOps
- Sales tactics and strategies
- Automated lead qualification tools
- Optimizing sales funnel strategies

**Cross-functional implications:**
- **Product marketing:** These findings can change website copy
- **Content marketing:** These can change the editorial calendar
- **Demand gen:** These can change what gets promoted
- **Brand marketing:** These can change where you need to be cited, mentioned, explained, and remembered

## Insight #3: Hidden Gems Beat Obvious Websites

Top-affinity websites are what you'd expect: SaaStr, Sales Hacker, Clari, Gong, Salesloft.

**But the Hidden Gems show more interesting opportunity:**
- Predictable Revenue: Low mainstream traffic, high relevance, potentially much better fit for this specific audience
- These are the places where competitors aren't yet competing

**Podcasts with strong affinity:**
- Strategy Sprints
- CEO Sales Strategies
- The B2B Revenue Executive Experience
- Revenue Leaders
- The RevOps Review

These are sponsored-newsletter, guest-post, and webinar-partner targets where your competitors might not yet be competing.

## The 30-Minute Exercise

Pull an audience research report. Take the top sources from Websites, Podcasts, Social Networks, Search & AI Tools, Keywords, and Topics. Compare against your current plan:

- Where are you already spending?
- Where are you creating content?
- Where are you trying to earn mentions?
- Where are you buying placements?
- Where are you completely absent?

**How different roles would use this:**

**Demand gen marketer:** Might realize the plan is too LinkedIn-heavy → pilot newsletter sponsorships, podcast placements, community-based partnerships

**Content marketer:** Build content around "AI in sales," "revenue operations best practices," "b2b sales metrics," "automated lead qualification tools" — because this audience is already signaling interest

**Partnership marketer:** Look at hidden-gem sites and podcasts before chasing the biggest, most obvious industry names

**Brand marketer:** Stop treating AI visibility as an SEO side quest → ask: when this audience asks Claude or ChatGPT about our category, do we show up as a credible answer?

**Product marketer:** Use keyword and topic language to rewrite landing page copy so it sounds less like internal positioning and more like how buyers actually describe their problems

**CEO/CMO:** Are we spending against the channels we're used to, or the places our audience actually learns, searches, and makes decisions?
