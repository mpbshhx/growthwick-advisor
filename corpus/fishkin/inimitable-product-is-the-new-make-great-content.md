# Inimitable Product is the New "Make Great Content"

By Rand Fishkin, SparkToro

## The Death of "Make Great Content"

For 25 years, Google told websites to "just make great content," and they'd sort out the rest. But no more.

Google's plan is now:
- Crawl and index the web's content
- Use mafia-like power to force a prisoner's dilemma paradox (if you don't let them index, they'll take it from someone else and reward them with scraps of traffic)
- Use that content to train their models/power their answers
- Make your work a redundant commodity
- Only send clicks when businesses either: A) Pay for ads, or B) Offer something Google can't provide directly in their AI-powered answer interface

## Two Solutions for Businesses and Creators

### Solution 1 (Long-term): Collective political action
- Support politicians who'd empower DOJ and FTC
- Break up tech monopolies
- Make self-preferencing illegal
- Cynically: won't happen anytime soon

### Solution 2 (Short-term): Build Inimitable Products

AI cannot:
- Invent ultrasonic chef's knives
- Tailor a made-to-measure suit
- Thoughtfully curate gift boxes from local makers
- Paint scenes with the colors of time
- Hand-carve tiny creatures
- Mow your lawn or fix your roof

To make inimitable products:
1. Uncover the overlap between what you're passionately capable of creating, what other solutions fail to offer, and what people in that market desperately want
2. Create it designed for Zero Click Marketing: can be shown via short-form video, amplified with few images, easily described by a rabid fan, made enticing through a brief summary atop an AI-answer
3. Get the word out through channels where your audience pays attention: social media, PR, advertising, search/AI answers, and word-of-mouth

## Is Content Marketing Dead?

Not fully declared dead — but the writing's on the wall. Only a fool would double down on "make great content" as the path forward.

**Near-universal advice:** 
- Ignore traffic
- Make inimitable products
- Shift priorities from "great content" on your own site to "great marketing" on the platforms where your audience pays attention

**The new funnel:**
- Influence is the new traffic
- Impressions and engagement are the new top of funnel
- Branded search volume is the new middle
- Sales remains the true measure of success

**The brutal takeaway:** Inimitable products may be defensible moats. Inimitable content is not. Content as product is dying.

## Why Still Publish?

- To influence search engines and AI tools (who still need sources to train off)
- To have a home base for an idea that can be cited and quoted and referred back to
- To have a place for the most loyal fans to access content in its intended format
- To distribute via email — one of the last owned channels
- To exercise longer-form creation that illustrates deeper thinking

Expected reach: <5,000 visits, <500 from search, <100 who read every word. But if they're the right ones, and it creates external influence beyond that, it may still be worthwhile.
