# Audience Research Brief: How We'd Market to Mid-Market RevOps Leaders

**Author: Rand Fishkin**

Audience research is the heart of it all. We use different words for it — customer insight, positioning, voice of customer, ICP development — but a lot of it comes down to the same questions:

- What does this audience care about?
- Where do they spend time online?
- How do they talk about their problems?
- Who do they listen to?
- What are they searching for?
- And what would actually help them make a decision?

This week's example: **mid-market RevOps leaders at B2B SaaS companies.**

A typical campaign for this audience might start with LinkedIn ABM, a Pavilion sponsorship, and a few blog posts around revenue operations. But how would this actually get done?

We ran a SparkToro report for this audience and asked: Where does this audience actually spend time, pay attention, search, listen, and learn — and where might marketers be missing them?

**The short answer:** LinkedIn matters. But it's not the whole story.

## Insight #1: LinkedIn is obvious. The better question is where they learn.

80.3% of this audience uses LinkedIn monthly, over-indexing by 15% compared with the US average.

But the more interesting part isn't just which platforms show up — it's what those platforms tell us about the audience's work habits, information diet, and decision-making environment.

**Slack:** 33.2% of this audience uses Slack monthly, over-indexing by 201% vs. the US average. This likely signals they work in fast-moving, cross-functional environments where internal conversations shape decisions.

A useful RevOps asset is not just something that gets clicked. It's something a VP of Revenue can drop into Slack with a note like, "This is exactly what I've been trying to explain," or "Can we talk about this in our team meeting on Monday?"

Assets worth creating:
- Benchmark reports
- Teardowns of common RevOps mistakes
- Calculators or diagnostics
- Spicy-but-useful points of view
- Workflow templates
- Comparison guides that make an internal debate easier

Slack may not be the channel you market in. But it could be the place your best content gets forwarded through.

**Substack:** 33.0% monthly, over-indexing by 44% — newsletter sponsorships, guest essays, creator partnerships, and earned mentions may be worth testing.

The big question: Where does this audience find ideas they trust enough to repeat internally?

## Insight #2: This audience is on Claude. A lot.

Roughly one in three people in this audience uses Claude, and they over-index by **438.4%** compared to the US average.

This audience also over-indexes on ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Perplexity. Most likely, this buyer is already asking AI tools questions before they ever get to your website.

**What this audience prompts AI for:** Best practices, sales tactics, automated lead qualification tools, and optimizing sales funnel strategies. They're looking for strategies, playbooks, and tactics.

Implications:
- **Product marketing:** These findings can change website copy
- **Content marketing:** Can change the editorial calendar
- **Demand gen:** Can change what gets promoted
- **Brand marketing:** Can change where you need to be cited, mentioned, explained, and remembered

The better question isn't just "where is this audience online?" It's: **"When this audience asks Claude or ChatGPT about our category, do we show up as a credible answer?"**

## Insight #3: The obvious websites are useful. The Hidden Gems are where I'd look harder.

Top-affinity websites as expected: SaaStr, Sales Hacker, Clari, Gong, Salesloft.

**Hidden Gems to look at harder:**
- **Predictable Revenue:** Low mainstream traffic, high relevance to this specific audience. Not the biggest platform. Possibly a much better fit.

**Podcast affinity:** Strategy Sprints, CEO Sales Strategies, The B2B Revenue Executive Experience, Revenue Leaders, The RevOps Review.

These are sponsored-newsletter, guest-post, and webinar-partner targets where your competitors might not yet be competing.

## What We'd Actually Do With This

Pull an audience research report. Compare top sources from Websites, Podcasts, Social Networks, Search & AI Tools, Keywords, and Topics against your current plan.

- Where are you already spending?
- Where are you creating content?
- Where are you trying to earn mentions?
- Where are you buying placements?
- Where are you completely absent?

**By role:**
- **Demand gen marketer:** Pilot newsletter sponsorships, podcast placements, or community-based partnerships
- **Content marketer:** Build content around "AI in sales," "revenue operations best practices," "b2b sales metrics," "automated lead qualification tools"
- **Partnership marketer:** Look at hidden-gem sites and podcasts before chasing the biggest industry names
- **Brand marketer:** Stop treating AI visibility as an SEO side quest — when this audience asks Claude or ChatGPT about your category, do you show up?
- **Product marketer:** Rewrite landing page copy using keyword and topic language that sounds like how buyers actually describe their problems
- **CEO or CMO:** Are we spending against the channels we're used to, or the places our audience actually learns, searches, and makes decisions?
