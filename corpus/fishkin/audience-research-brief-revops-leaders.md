# Audience Research Brief: How We'd Market to Mid-Market RevOps Leaders

By Rand Fishkin / SparkToro

## The Core Question

"Where does this audience actually spend time, pay attention, search, listen, and learn — and where might marketers be missing them?"

## Insight #1: LinkedIn Is Obvious. The Better Question Is Where They Learn.

- 80.3% use LinkedIn monthly (over-index 15% vs. US average) — expected
- **Slack: 33.2% use it, over-index by 201%** — likely their company chat, but signals they work in fast-moving, cross-functional environments where internal conversations shape decisions

**Key implication:** You can't market in their company Slack — but you can create things worth bringing into those conversations. A useful RevOps asset gets dropped into Slack with "this is exactly what I've been trying to explain." 

The most shareable RevOps assets:
- Benchmark reports
- Teardowns of common RevOps mistakes
- Calculators or diagnostics
- Spicy-but-useful points of view
- Workflow templates
- Comparison guides that make internal debates easier

**Substack:** 33.0% use it monthly, over-index by 44% → newsletter sponsorships, guest essays, creator partnerships, earned mentions worth testing.

"If your whole strategy is LinkedIn-first because that's where B2B buyers are, you may be missing the more useful question: Where does this audience find ideas they trust enough to repeat internally?"

## Insight #2: This Audience Is on Claude. A Lot.

- ~1 in 3 people in this audience uses Claude
- **Over-index by 438.4% compared to US average**
- Also over-indexes on ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Perplexity

This buyer is asking AI tools questions before they ever get to your website.

**AI prompt topics:** Best practices, sales tactics, automated lead qualification tools, optimizing sales funnel strategies, playbooks and tactics.

Marketing implications:
- Product marketing: changes website copy
- Content marketing: changes editorial calendar
- Demand gen: changes what gets promoted
- Brand marketing: changes where you need to be cited, mentioned, explained, and remembered

## Insight #3: Hidden Gems Are Where I'd Look Harder

**Obvious top-affinity websites:** SaaStr, Sales Hacker, Clari, Gong, Salesloft (expected)

**Hidden gems:** Predictable Revenue — low mainstream traffic, high relevance to this specific audience

**High-affinity podcasts:** Strategy Sprints, CEO Sales Strategies, The B2B Revenue Executive Experience, Revenue Leaders, The RevOps Review

→ These are sponsored-newsletter, guest-post, and webinar-partner targets where competitors might not yet be competing.

## What We'd Actually Do With This

30-minute exercise: Pull an audience research report, compare top sources against your current plan. Ask:
- Where are you already spending?
- Where are you creating content?
- Where are you trying to earn mentions?
- Where are you completely absent?

By role:
- **Demand gen:** Reallocate some budget from LinkedIn to newsletter sponsorships, podcast placements, community-based partnerships
- **Content marketer:** Build around "AI in sales," "revenue operations best practices," "automated lead qualification tools"
- **Partnership marketer:** Target hidden-gem podcasts before chasing the biggest names
- **Brand marketer:** Stop treating AI visibility as SEO side quest — ask "when this audience asks Claude about our category, do we show up?"
- **Product marketer:** Rewrite landing page copy in language buyers actually use to describe their problems

**The CEO/CMO question:** "Are we spending against the channels we're used to, or the places our audience actually learns, searches, and makes decisions?"

Audience research is not just for content ideas. It's the foundation every marketing function plans against. A single SparkToro report can change: a paid media plan, a podcast strategy, a partner list, a product marketing brief, a webinar calendar, and a board-slide explanation for budget reallocation.
