## Dave Gerhardt's Marketing Philosophy

### 1. Brand is a Growth Driver, Not a Cost Center
Most B2B companies treat brand as soft/fluffy. DG argues brand is a compounding asset:
- Reduces CAC over time (people come to you already knowing who you are)
- Improves conversion rates (warm traffic converts better than cold)
- Creates pricing power (people pay more for brands they trust)

### 2. The Founder Brand Thesis
In early-stage B2B companies, the founder's personal brand IS the company brand.

Key claims:
- Founders with strong personal brands close deals faster
- Investor interest is higher
- Recruiting is easier
- Media coverage comes to you rather than requiring PR spend

**How to build a founder brand:**
- Pick one primary channel (LinkedIn for B2B)
- Share what you're learning and doing — process, not polish
- Consistency over time beats perfection
- Distribution is the discipline: show up every day, build the audience, then everything else gets easier

### 3. Marketing is a Distribution Problem
Most B2B companies have decent products but terrible distribution. The default is: build product → write some content → hope it gets found.

DG's approach: distribution first. Build the audience, then sell to them.
- Email list = owned audience
- Community = compounding engagement
- Podcast = intimate relationship at scale
- LinkedIn = daily touchpoints

### 4. Content Should Do Real Work
Not "content marketing" as a checkbox — content that actually helps buyers decide:
- Use cases they recognize themselves in
- Objection-handling built into the content
- Social proof woven throughout
- Clear next step at every touchpoint

### 5. The Pipeline Isn't the Only Metric That Matters
Attribution-obsessed B2B marketing creates the wrong incentives:
- Optimizes for the easily measurable, not the actually impactful
- Brand, community, and word-of-mouth don't show up in last-click attribution
- Dark funnel matters — most B2B buyers have already decided before they fill out a form

## Core Frameworks

### The B2B Marketing Flywheel
1. Build an audience (owned + earned)
2. Create content that helps that audience
3. Convert a fraction to customers
4. Customers become advocates
5. Advocates expand the audience
6. Repeat

### Demand Generation vs. Lead Generation (DG's take)
- **Lead gen:** Fill out this form to get this content. Pay-to-play with attention. Mostly transactional.
- **Demand gen:** Build awareness, build preference, be top-of-mind when the buyer is ready to buy.

DG argues most B2B companies do too much lead gen and too little demand gen. The result: marketing that generates MQLs but not revenue.

### The Dark Funnel
Most B2B buying decisions happen before the official "funnel" begins:
- Buyers research in private Slack communities, LinkedIn DMs, peer conversations
- They've often already decided who they want to buy from before they fill out any form
- Marketing that only tracks form fills misses the majority of influence

Implication: invest in brand, community, and organic presence — even if you can't directly attribute it.
