# Demand Generation in B2B

Source: https://www.exitfive.com/blog/demand-generation (fetch blocked — compiled from Exit Five and DG public content)

## What Demand Generation Actually Means

Demand generation is misunderstood in B2B. Most people think it means "generate leads." It doesn't.

**Demand generation = creating awareness and preference BEFORE buyers are actively shopping.**

The goal is to be the company a buyer thinks of when they finally have budget and urgency. You can't create that awareness the week a buyer decides to purchase — you had to build it months or years earlier.

---

## Demand Gen vs. Lead Gen: The Distinction That Matters

| | Demand Generation | Lead Generation |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Build awareness + preference | Capture contact info |
| **Timing** | Before buyer is in-market | When buyer might be in-market |
| **Content** | Free, ungated, valuable | Gated behind a form |
| **Measurement** | Brand metrics, dark funnel | MQLs, form fills |
| **Compounding** | Yes — builds over time | No — turns off when you stop |
| **Competitive moat** | High — hard to copy | Low — table stakes |

Most B2B companies do too much lead gen and too little demand gen. Result: high MQL volumes with poor conversion, because leads were captured before genuine interest was established.

## The Three Types of Demand

### 1. Demand You Create
Buyers had the problem but weren't aware of your category as a solution.

**Example:** When Drift launched "conversational marketing," most companies didn't have a live chat budget line. Drift had to create the category before they could capture demand for it.

**How to create demand:**
- Thought leadership that names a problem buyers recognize
- Content that helps buyers understand why the old way isn't working
- Events, podcasts, and community that shape how buyers think

### 2. Demand You Capture
Buyers are actively in-market. They're comparing options.

**How to capture demand:**
- Paid search
- SEO for category + solution keywords
- G2/Capterra presence
- Sales motions

### 3. Demand You Accelerate
Buyers know the problem, know the category, but haven't prioritized it yet.

**How to accelerate demand:**
- Email nurture
- Retargeting
- Case studies that increase urgency
- ROI calculators
- Events that get buyers to commit to timelines

## The Dark Funnel Problem

The most important insight in modern B2B demand generation: **most buying decisions are made before the official funnel begins.**

**What happens in the dark funnel:**
- Buyers ask peers in Slack communities for recommendations
- They search LinkedIn for what people at their company tier are using
- They read reviews on G2 and Capterra
- They talk to other founders/practitioners they trust
- They watch the vendor's content for months before engaging

**By the time a buyer fills out a form:**
- They've already shortlisted you (or not)
- They've already formed a preference
- They often already know who they're going to pick

**Implication:** The form fill is not the start of the journey. It's near the end. Marketing that only tracks form fills is measuring the last 5% of the influence and ignoring the other 95%.

**What to do about it:**
- Invest in brand, community, and thought leadership (even if you can't track it)
- Survey closed-won customers: "How did you first hear about us? What convinced you?"
- Track branded search as a proxy for dark funnel influence
- Build presence in the communities where your buyers actually talk

## What Good Demand Gen Looks Like

### Content Strategy
- Ungated content that is genuinely useful to your ICP
- Written, video, and audio formats (buyers consume differently)
- Distribution to where buyers already are (don't make them find you)
- Consistency over time (one great piece rarely moves the needle; 100 decent pieces over 18 months does)

### Channel Strategy
**LinkedIn:** The highest-leverage channel for B2B demand gen in 2024. Your ICP is already there, in a professional mindset, open to business content.

**Email/Newsletter:** Owned audience. Compounding asset. Most reliable channel for nurturing long-sales-cycle buyers.

**Podcast:** Intimate relationship at scale. Listeners spend 30-60 minutes with you regularly. Hard to replicate with any other format.

**Community:** Creates peer-to-peer advocacy. Members recommend each other's products. Lowest CAC for products that earn it.

### What NOT to Do
- Don't gate your best content behind forms (it signals distrust and reduces reach)
- Don't measure demand gen purely on MQL volume (wrong metric for what demand gen does)
- Don't stop when the pipeline looks short-term healthy (demand gen is a long-game investment)
- Don't run demand gen in isolation from sales (alignment required for the handoff to work)

## Dave Gerhardt's Core Demand Gen Principle

> "Distribution is the discipline. You can have the best marketing content in the world, but if nobody sees it, it doesn't exist. Build the audience first. Then everything else gets easier."

The marketers who win long-term are the ones who invest in building audiences when they don't immediately need them.
