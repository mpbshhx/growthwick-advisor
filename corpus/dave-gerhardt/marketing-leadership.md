# B2B Marketing Leadership

Source: https://www.exitfive.com/blog/marketing-leadership (fetch blocked — compiled from Exit Five and DG public content)

## What Marketing Leadership Actually Is

Most marketers become marketing leaders by being good at tactics. Then they discover that leadership is an entirely different skill set.

**Tactics:** Creating campaigns, writing copy, running ads, building content
**Leadership:** Setting strategy, managing a team, reporting to the board, making resource allocation decisions, aligning with sales

The transition from practitioner to leader is where most B2B marketers get stuck.

## What a VP of Marketing / CMO Actually Does

### 1. Sets the Strategy
Not "we should do more content." Actual strategy:
- Who is the ICP and why?
- What is the primary marketing motion (product-led, sales-assisted, community-led)?
- Which channels will we own vs. ignore?
- What does the funnel look like and where does marketing hand off to sales?

### 2. Allocates the Budget
Marketing leadership is fundamentally a resource allocation function.
- How much goes to brand vs. performance?
- How much goes to top-of-funnel vs. bottom?
- What experiments are worth running?
- What is the ROI expectation for each investment?

### 3. Manages and Develops the Team
Great marketing leaders build teams that outlast them.
- Hire people who complement your weaknesses
- Give them clear ownership and accountability
- Remove blockers rather than solve every problem yourself
- Develop people's skills and careers, not just outputs

### 4. Represents Marketing to the Business
The marketing leader translates marketing work into business terms:
- Pipeline contribution
- Revenue influence
- CAC trends
- Brand health metrics

If you can't explain what marketing is doing in terms the CEO and CFO understand, you will lose budget battles.

### 5. Aligns Marketing with Sales
The most important operational relationship in B2B marketing is with sales. When it's broken, leads go nowhere and blame is everywhere.

Good marketing-sales alignment includes:
- Agreed ICP definition (marketing targets the same buyer sales wants to close)
- Agreed lead definition (when does a lead become an MQL? When does an MQL become an SQL?)
- Regular pipeline reviews (not just reporting, but collaborative pipeline building)
- Content that sales actually uses

## The CMO Career Path (DG's Perspective)

### Most CMOs Are Set Up to Fail

The average CMO tenure at a US company is 28 months. Why?
- Unrealistic expectations (board wants results in 6 months, brand takes 18)
- Misalignment with CEO on what marketing is supposed to do
- No clear ICP going into the role
- Marketing hired to fix a sales problem (can't be done)

### How to Succeed as a First-Time CMO

**Before you accept the role:**
1. Get clarity on what success looks like (is it pipeline? brand? PLG? customer expansion?)
2. Understand the ICP (if the company doesn't have one, that's a project)
3. Meet the sales leader (if you can't work together, don't take the job)
4. Assess the team you're inheriting (who stays, who goes, who needs to be hired?)
5. Get clear on the budget and decision rights (can you actually do the job?)

**In the first 90 days:**
1. Listen before acting (don't come in with a new strategy on day 3)
2. Talk to 20+ customers (not sales, not the team — actual customers)
3. Talk to 10+ lost deals (why didn't they buy? Who did they choose instead?)
4. Map the current funnel (what exists, what's working, what's broken?)
5. Identify the 2-3 highest leverage things to fix first

**Months 3-12:**
1. Fix what's most broken
2. Start building what's most missing
3. Report results clearly and proactively (don't make leadership ask)
4. Build credibility with sales (they are your most important internal stakeholders)

## Building a Marketing Team

### Hire For Stage
Early-stage (0-5 person marketing team) needs generalists and hustlers.
Growth-stage (5-15 person team) needs specialists with enough range to collaborate.
Scale stage (15+ person team) needs deep specialists and leaders who can manage.

Hiring a specialist before you need them = expensive and underutilized.
Hiring a generalist at scale stage = underpowered for the complexity.

### The First Three Hires (DG's Framework)
For most B2B companies at Series A/B:
1. **Demand gen / growth** → drives pipeline
2. **Content / brand** → builds the engine for long-term
3. **Marketing ops** → makes sure you can measure what matters

Hire in that order. Don't hire a creative director before you have a demand gen function.

### Avoid These Hiring Mistakes
- Hiring senior titles before you need them (VP at 5 person company is often a mistake)
- Hiring for channel expertise without strategic thinking
- Overlooking data and analytical skills in a creative candidate
- Hiring people like you instead of people who complement you

## Marketing-Sales Alignment: The Real Work

The #1 complaint from B2B CMOs: sales doesn't use what marketing creates.
The #1 complaint from B2B sales leaders: marketing generates leads that don't convert.

Both complaints are usually partially true. The fix is structural, not political.

**The alignment framework:**
1. **Shared ICP definition** (monthly review at minimum)
2. **Shared pipeline reviews** (marketing and sales in the same room looking at the same data)
3. **Clear lead SLAs** (what happens to an MQL within X hours/days)
4. **Feedback loops** (sales tells marketing why leads didn't close; marketing adjusts targeting)
5. **Shared wins** (celebrate closed deals from marketing-generated pipeline publicly)

**DG's principle:** If sales and marketing are in different buildings (physically or organizationally), you've already failed at alignment. Find ways to sit together, review together, and win together.
