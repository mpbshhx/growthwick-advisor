# Content Marketing for B2B

Source: https://www.exitfive.com/blog/content-marketing (fetch blocked — compiled from Exit Five and DG public content)

## Why Most B2B Content Marketing Doesn't Work

The reason most B2B content fails isn't quality. It's distribution.

Companies publish content, share it once on LinkedIn, and wonder why it doesn't generate pipeline. The content itself is often fine. But without a distribution strategy, content is a tree falling in the woods with nobody around.

**The content marketing equation:**
Content × Distribution = Results

Most companies overinvest in content and underinvest in distribution.

---

## What Content Marketing Is Actually For

Content marketing serves two distinct functions:

### 1. Demand Creation (Top-of-Funnel)
Goal: build awareness and brand preference with buyers who are not yet in-market.
- They have the problem but aren't actively shopping
- They might not even know your category exists
- You're building the relationship before the sales conversation

**Formats:** LinkedIn posts, podcasts, newsletters, ungated articles

### 2. Demand Capture (Bottom-of-Funnel)
Goal: help buyers in-market make a decision in your favor.
- They're evaluating options
- They need proof, context, and objection handling
- You're giving them what they need to say yes

**Formats:** Case studies, product docs, comparison pages, ROI calculators, implementation guides

Most B2B content libraries are heavy on fluffy top-of-funnel content and light on bottom-of-funnel proof. This is backwards — bottom-of-funnel content has the highest direct revenue impact.

## The Content Strategy Framework (DG's Approach)

### 1. Start with Audience, Not Topics
Who are you writing for? What do they care about? What are they struggling with?

**Don't:** Brainstorm topics you find interesting.
**Do:** Talk to customers and prospects. Read their questions on LinkedIn, in communities, in support tickets. Write content that answers questions they're actually asking.

### 2. Build a Pillar + Distribution System
**Pillar content:** 1 long-form piece per week (podcast episode, long article, video)

**Distribution assets derived from pillar:**
- 3-5 LinkedIn posts per week (excerpts, insights, takes)
- 1 newsletter section
- 1-2 tweets
- Pull quotes for social

You're not creating more content — you're getting more mileage from the same content.

### 3. Own Your Distribution Channels
Rented vs. owned channels:
- **Rented:** LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube (algorithm can kill reach overnight)
- **Owned:** Email list, community, podcast subscribers

Strategy: use rented channels to build owned channels. LinkedIn to newsletter. Newsletter to community. Community to customers.

The email list is the most valuable asset in B2B content marketing. It's yours. You can email it regardless of algorithm changes.

### 4. Be Consistent Over 18+ Months
Most B2B content programs get killed in months 3-6 when results aren't showing.

DG's thesis: content compounds like a savings account. The early deposits feel insignificant. After 18-24 months, the compounding becomes visible. After 3 years, it's a competitive moat.

**What to track in months 1-6:** Email subscribers, LinkedIn follower growth, content engagement rates
**What to track in months 6-18:** Organic search traffic, inbound pipeline from content, brand mentions
**What to track in months 18+:** CAC reduction, % of pipeline from organic/brand channels, community size

## B2B Content Formats That Actually Work

### LinkedIn Posts (Highest Leverage for B2B)
The most efficient format for B2B demand generation in 2024.

**Why:** Your ICP is already there, in a professional mindset, scrolling for ideas.

**What works:**
- "Hot takes" with a clear point of view
- Behind-the-scenes content (what you're building, what you're learning)
- Frameworks with visual structure (bullet points, numbered lists)
- Stories with a lesson
- Honest takes on failures and what you learned

**What doesn't work:**
- Corporate announcements (nobody cares)
- Blog post links without context ("New post on our blog!" generates zero engagement)
- Generic "thought leadership" that could have been written by anyone

### Email Newsletter
The highest-converting channel for B2B content marketing.

**Why:** Subscribers opted in. They expect your content. It's personal.

**What works:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly consistency
- 1 key idea per send (not a content dump)
- Written in a conversational voice, not a corporate one
- Clear next action at the end

**Format:** Plain text email with a short insight + one CTA often outperforms designed HTML newsletters

### Podcast
The most underutilized channel in B2B.

**Why it works:** 30-60 minutes with your audience, every week, in their earbuds. No other format creates that level of trust and intimacy.

**The B2B podcast playbook:**
1. Interview your ICP (practitioners and leaders in your target market)
2. They share with their audience → distribution you didn't have before
3. Listeners become familiar with your brand through regular exposure
4. Some convert to customers (often months after first listen)

**ROI:** Hardest to attribute but often the highest-quality pipeline.

## Content + Community: The Compounding Combo

Content builds an audience. Community activates that audience.

**The feedback loop:**
1. Content attracts your ICP
2. Community converts passive readers to active participants
3. Community generates user-generated content (discussions, insights, questions)
4. User-generated content informs your content strategy
5. Better content grows the community
6. Loop compounds

Exit Five is DG's proof case: content (newsletter + podcast) drives community membership, community drives content topics, content drives distribution, distribution drives more subscribers.

## Dave Gerhardt's Content Principles

1. **Distribution > Creation** — if nobody sees it, it doesn't matter how good it is
2. **Own your audience** — email list is non-negotiable
3. **Have a POV** — bland content generates no brand
4. **Consistency beats brilliance** — showing up every week for 2 years beats one viral post
5. **Serve buyers, not SEO bots** — write for humans first
6. **Repurpose everything** — one pillar piece = 10+ distribution assets
7. **Measure over long windows** — content ROI compounds; quarterly measurement is misleading
