# Exit Five Podcast

Source: https://www.exitfive.com/podcast (fetch blocked — compiled from publicly known information)

## About the Exit Five Podcast

The Exit Five Podcast is the flagship audio content from Dave Gerhardt and Exit Five. Weekly conversations with B2B marketing practitioners and leaders.

**Format:** Interview-based (mostly) + solo episodes
**Length:** 30-60 minutes
**Cadence:** Weekly
**Distribution:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, major podcast platforms

---

## What the Podcast Covers

The podcast focuses on practical B2B marketing — what actually works, not theory.

**Primary topic areas:**
- Demand generation strategies
- Brand building in B2B
- Content marketing that drives pipeline
- Building marketing teams and managing up
- CMO career lessons and mistakes
- Sales and marketing alignment
- Community-led growth
- Marketing operations and measurement
- AI and marketing workflows

---

## Why DG Built the Podcast

DG's thesis on podcasting in B2B:

> "A podcast is the highest-trust content format. When someone listens to your podcast regularly, they spend 45 minutes with you every week. That's an intimacy and relationship depth you can't build any other way."

For Exit Five, the podcast serves as:
1. **Demand generation** — reaches new B2B marketers who discover it through Spotify/Apple recommendations
2. **Brand building** — each episode is a brand touchpoint with the guest's audience
3. **Community driver** — podcast listeners become the most engaged community members
4. **Content flywheel** — each episode generates clips, newsletter content, LinkedIn posts

---

## Guest Selection Philosophy

Exit Five podcast guests are primarily practitioners, not celebrities.

The priority is: people who are actually doing the work and can share specific, tactically useful insights.

**Typical guests:**
- VPs of Marketing at B2B SaaS companies ($10M-$500M ARR)
- CMOs at growth-stage B2B companies
- Demand gen and content marketing leaders
- Marketing operations experts
- Founders who are building marketing-led companies

**Not typical:** Influencer marketers, B2C CMOs, people promoting a new book with no tactical depth

## The Podcast as a Business Model Component

Exit Five's revenue model is a proof case for the B2B podcast strategy:

1. Podcast builds audience
2. Audience joins newsletter
3. Newsletter readers join the paid community ($400/year)
4. Community generates sponsorship revenue
5. Sponsors are B2B companies reaching Exit Five's audience

The podcast is the engine. Without the podcast, the distribution for everything else is weaker.

---

## What to Take from the Exit Five Podcast Model

For B2B companies considering a podcast:

1. **Pick a specific audience** — not "marketing" but "B2B marketing leaders at Series B companies"
2. **Guest quality > production quality** — a great conversation on decent audio beats a bad conversation on perfect audio
3. **Cross-promote to your other channels** — every episode should generate 5+ other assets
4. **Think 2-year timeline** — podcast audiences compound slowly; most shows quit in month 6
5. **The guest IS the distribution** — when you feature someone, they share it with their audience; that's your primary growth channel early on
6. **Have a clear editorial POV** — Exit Five's POV is "B2B marketing is broken and this is how to fix it." Every episode connects back to that.
