# B2B Marketing Strategy

## The Problem with Most B2B Marketing Strategies

Most B2B companies have a decent product and a terrible distribution strategy. The default playbook:
1. Build product
2. Write some blog posts
3. Run some ads
4. Hope the pipeline comes

This is not a strategy. This is activity masquerading as strategy.

---

## What B2B Marketing Strategy Actually Is

**Marketing strategy = a clear answer to four questions:**
1. Who are we selling to? (ICP)
2. What problem do we solve for them, uniquely?
3. Where do we reach them before they're in-market?
4. How do we convert the demand we create?

Most companies have sloppy answers to questions 1 and 2, no answer to question 3, and an over-indexed answer to question 4.

## The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): The Foundation

Every marketing strategy starts with ICP clarity. Without it, you're broadcasting to everyone and converting no one.

**ICP is not a persona.** A persona is a profile of a hypothetical person. An ICP is the characteristics of companies and buyers where you win consistently, generate value, and retain.

**ICP components for B2B:**
- Company size (employees/revenue)
- Industry or vertical
- Tech stack or infrastructure signals
- Growth stage
- Organizational structure (who owns the problem?)
- Pain acuity (do they feel this pain urgently?)
- Buying power (can they buy?)

**Finding your ICP:**
1. Look at your best 10-20 customers
2. What do they have in common?
3. Those commonalities = your ICP
4. Validate with churned customers (who did you lose, and why?)

## The B2B Marketing Flywheel

Most B2B companies think in funnels. DG thinks in flywheels.

**Funnel thinking:** Awareness → Consideration → Decision → closed.
Linear. Each prospect starts at the top and gets pushed through.

**Flywheel thinking:**
1. Build audience (newsletter, community, LinkedIn, podcast)
2. Create content that serves that audience
3. Build brand and trust over time
4. Some fraction converts when they're ready
5. Happy customers become advocates
6. Advocates bring more audience
7. Flywheel compounds

The flywheel rewards patience. Most companies want leads now. The flywheel delivers CAC efficiency, better conversion rates, and compounding growth over time.

## The Three Horizons of B2B Marketing

### Horizon 1: Capture Existing Demand (Now)
People already know they have the problem and are actively shopping.
- Paid search (keywords with purchase intent)
- SEO for high-intent terms
- Retargeting
- Sales-assisted close

This is where most B2B marketing budgets go. It's competitive and gets more expensive over time.

### Horizon 2: Convert Consideration (3-12 months)
People know they have the problem but aren't actively shopping yet.
- Email nurture
- Content marketing (middle-of-funnel)
- Webinars
- Case studies

### Horizon 3: Create Demand (12+ months)
People don't yet know they have the problem, or haven't prioritized solving it.
- Brand marketing
- Community building
- Thought leadership content
- Podcast
- LinkedIn presence

Most companies underinvest in Horizon 3. The companies that win long-term balance investment across all three.

## Why Brand Is a B2B Growth Strategy

DG's most contrarian take: brand is not soft. Brand is the growth lever most B2B companies ignore while complaining about rising CAC.

**What brand does:**
- Reduces CAC (buyers come to you pre-sold)
- Increases conversion rates (warm is always better than cold)
- Creates pricing power (pay more for brands you trust)
- Improves retention (customers stay with brands they identify with)
- Enables word-of-mouth (you can't engineer word-of-mouth without brand)

**What brand requires:**
- Consistency over time
- A distinct point of view
- Willingness to exclude (brand that appeals to everyone appeals to no one)
- Patient capital

## The Founder Brand as Marketing Strategy

In early-stage B2B:
- The founder IS the brand
- The founder's audience IS the distribution channel
- The founder's credibility IS the sales close

**How to build a founder brand that drives company growth:**
1. Pick one primary channel (LinkedIn for B2B)
2. Post consistently — daily or near-daily
3. Share what you're actually doing and learning
4. Document the journey, don't just polish the wins
5. Engage genuinely — reply, comment, DM
6. Let it compound over 12-18 months minimum

**The payoff:**
- Inbound pipeline (people reach out after seeing your content)
- Lower sales cycle (they already trust you when they schedule the call)
- PR and media interest (journalists follow thought leaders)
- Recruiting (top talent wants to work with recognized leaders)

## Measurement: What to Track

**Short-term (operational):**
- MQLs, SQLs, conversion rates
- CAC by channel
- Pipeline created by source

**Medium-term (leading indicators):**
- Email list growth rate
- Community engagement
- Website traffic from brand terms
- Share of voice vs. competitors

**Long-term (brand health):**
- Net Promoter Score
- Organic/word-of-mouth pipeline %
- Brand recognition in ICP surveys

**The dark funnel caveat:**
Most B2B buying research happens in places you can't track:
- LinkedIn DMs
- Private Slack communities
- Peer recommendations
- Conference conversations

Don't optimize only for what's measurable. Invest in what drives the dark funnel too.
