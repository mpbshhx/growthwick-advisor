# Four Fits For $100M+ Growth — Brian Balfour

## The Core Insight: Smooth Sailers vs. Tugboats

Brian Balfour observed two types of companies after building, advising, or investing in 40+ tech companies:

- **Smooth Sailers**: Growth comes easily, like guiding a boulder downhill. These companies grow despite organizational chaos, not executing "best" practices, and missing low-hanging fruit.
- **Tugboats**: Growth feels like pushing a boulder uphill. Despite best practices, great teams, and picking low-hanging fruit, they struggle.

The difference: The Four Fits Framework.

## The Four Fits

### 1. Market Product Fit
"The road to a $100M company doesn't start with product."

When people focus on product-market fit, they always focus on product first — but that's putting the cart before the horse. You must properly understand the problem and audience before thinking about solution.

**Key signals of Market Product Fit:**
- Qualitative indicators
- Quantitative indicators  
- Intuitive indicators

### 2. Product Channel Fit
"The fit that makes or breaks your growth strategy."

Common mistake: "We're focused on product-market fit right now. Once we have that we'll test a bunch of different channels." This assumes channels will mold to your product — but they won't.

**Core principle: Products are built to fit with channels. Channels do not mold to products.**

### 3. Channel Model Fit: The ARPU-CAC Danger Zone
Channels are determined by your model and vice versa.

The ARPU ↔ CAC spectrum: Plot almost all $100M companies on it perfectly.
- High ARPU = can afford expensive channels (sales, high-touch)
- Low ARPU = must use cheap/self-serve channels (SEO, viral, PLG)

### 4. Model Market Fit: The Threshold
Your market (and # of customers within it) influences your model.

**Simple math**: ARPU × Total Customers In Market × % You Think You Can Capture ≥ $100M

Many early-stage startups fail this math before they even start.

## Applying the Four Fits

**Key lessons:**
1. You need **all four fits** to grow to $100M+
2. You can't think about the four fits **in isolation** — they form an ecosystem
3. You need to **constantly revisit** the fits because they continuously change or break down

## Why Most Companies Fail at Moving Up or Down Market

Most companies fail when trying to change market segment because they only change one fit (usually product or model) without realizing all four fits need to adjust together.

## Case Study: HubSpot Sales Product

Brian applied the Four Fits framework as VP Growth at HubSpot, guiding the Sales product from $0 toward $100M. The framework helped navigate many twists and turns, keeping the strategy on path.
