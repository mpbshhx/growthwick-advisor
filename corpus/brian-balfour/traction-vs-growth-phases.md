# Traction vs Growth: The Three Phases of Growing a Startup

By Brian Balfour

## Overview

Every startup goes through three phases:
1. **Traction**
2. **Transition**
3. **Growth**

Goals, metrics, channels, focus, and team structure all evolve as you move through these phases. Knowing where you are helps you understand what to spend time on.

---

## Phase 1: Traction

### Goal
One and only goal: **find product-market fit among some audience segment.** Part of this is understanding how large that segment is to make sure you can build a compelling business.

### Metric
**Retention.** If your product doesn't retain users, there's no point in growing the top of the funnel.

### Volume
Just turn on the faucet and get a steady, consistent stream of users coming in the front door. Resources spent increasing volume past the level needed to understand PMF are wasted — they could be spent understanding retention.

Note: Your CPA will likely be greater than LTV at this stage. That's fine, as long as you're not putting runway at risk. Optimizing CPA and LTV comes after finding PMF.

### Channels
Try a few to find that steady stream, then focus in. Managing multiple channels creates overhead. With limited resources, keep overhead at a minimum. Avoid the "shotgun approach." Choose 2-3 channels as leading hypotheses.

### Optimization
Focus on **large macro optimizations.** Try big changes in messaging, user flow, target audience. Don't get caught in micro optimizations (button colors, word tweaks). Big changes → big insights.

### Team
One person leading growth (probably a founder), thinking about it 80%+ of their time. Probably needs part-time support from a developer and/or designer.

---

## Phase 2: Transition

The awkward teenage years. Growing pains. Start laying the foundation of a team, tools, and process.

### Goal
Identify, define, and understand the **growth levers** for your business. If success metric is DAUs, uncover the variables in product and marketing that lead to increases in DAUs.

### Metric
**Growth rate** on weekly or monthly basis — your guiding star from here on. Also start tracking CPA and LTV. Payback period should be under 3 months, otherwise you'll have cash flow issues.

### Volume
Start turning up the faucet. But pay close attention to retention as you do — retention may not hold as volume increases.

### Channels
**Do not diversify yet.** Focus. Identify the one channel with the most ceiling room and double down. The fastest way to increase growth rate: expand on something already working (until you've saturated the channel) rather than trying new channels you know nothing about.

"Most large companies get 80% of their growth from one channel anyway. Zynga → Facebook. LinkedIn → Virality (email). Instagram → Virality (sharing). HubSpot → Content Marketing. TripAdvisor → Search."

### Optimization
Still large macro optimizations during transition phase. As you move to growth stage, micro optimizations come into focus.

### Team
Build a dedicated growth team:
- One growth leader (PM or VP of Growth)
- At minimum: developer + designer
- Possible additions: data scientist, channel-specific resources (e.g., content marketer)

---

## Phase 3: Growth

Let's go.

### Goal
**Up and to the right.** Focus on your growth levers — tweak and crank them.

### Metric
Growth rate remains primary. But **payback period** comes into play. Payback period typically increases as a company grows — channels saturate, become more competitive, capital constraints loosen. The larger the payback period you can afford, the more channels and options available.

### Volume
If traction/transition was a faucet — growth is a **firehose.** Turn up volume as much as possible.

### Channels
Still 80%+ of growth from a primary channel. But by this stage:
- Growth process should be humming like a machine
- More capital and team resources available
- Primary channel may be saturating

Now is the time to diversify channels.

### Optimization
**Micro.** By now you know the big things that work for your audience. Small changes across a very large base can have meaningful results.

### Team
Growth team really expands:
- One growth executive
- Multiple growth PMs
- Each PM leads a team of developer, design, data, and channel-specific talent
