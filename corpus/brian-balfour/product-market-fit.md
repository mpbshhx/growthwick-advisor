## What Is Product Market Fit?

**Occurs when you build something that a meaningful segment of customers desperately wants.**

Marc Andreessen's definition: "You know you have it when the customers are buying the product just as fast as you can make it — or usage is growing just as fast as you can add more servers."

Classic indicator: You can feel PMF happening. The product spreads despite organizational chaos. The Smooth Sailor vs. Tugboat metaphor:
- **Smooth Sailors:** A little effort for lots of speed — grow despite organizational chaos
- **Tugboats:** A lot of effort for little speed — struggle to grow despite great execution

## How AI Disrupts Product Market Fit

### AI Increases Both Problem Space and Solution Space

You can now solve problems that couldn't previously be solved, and offer new solutions to old problems. The opportunity is larger. But the risks are also larger.

### Speed of PMF Loss Has Accelerated Dramatically

In the mobile era: years between new tech capability and customer expectations increasing.

In AI:
- New capabilities launched monthly or weekly
- Massive developer ecosystem already in place
- Distribution to hundreds of millions happened fast, cheap, and freely

**Result: Companies can lose Product Market Fit overnight.**

## Case Study: Chegg's PMF Collapse

One of the clearest examples of AI-driven PMF destruction:

- January 2024: $1.2 billion valuation
- October 2024: $150 million valuation (90% decline in 9 months)
- Lost half a million subscribers

**What Chegg did:** Homework help to students via company-generated, SEO-distributed content. The loop: more subscribers → more funding for content → more subscribers.

**What happened:** When OpenAI launched ChatGPT, students could get immediate, personalized, FREE answers. This broke Chegg's entire growth loop.

**The fatal timing problem:** Chegg launched first AI features 26 months after ChatGPT. In past technological transitions, that would have been fast enough. In the AI era, it was fatal.

## Markets AI Is Expanding vs. Contracting

**Expanding markets:**
- Canva: Design for everyone (democratizing creation)
- Lovable/Bolt: No-code app building (democratizing development)
- Cursor: AI-native coding (expanding who can be a developer)

**Contracting markets:**
- Chegg: Homework help (AI does it better, faster, free)
- Seat-based customer support tools: AI replaces human support agents, so seat count shrinks

## PMF in the AI Era: Key Questions to Ask

1. **Who is your customer?** New buyer personas have emerged. The traditional IT buyer focused on features and integrations. The AI buyer often comes from operations, evaluating against headcount savings and process automation — completely different approval process, budget sources, and implementation timelines.

2. **What is your moat if a competitor drops price 10x or offers it free?** This is now a live question for almost every software category.

3. **How fast could you lose PMF?** The Chegg timeline (9 months from $1.2B to $150M) should be your reference point.

4. **Are you expanding or contracting the addressable market?** AI can make markets 10x larger (Canva) or 10x smaller (Chegg).

5. **Does your PMF depend on distribution channels that AI is destroying?** SEO-dependent PMF is especially vulnerable.

## The Four Fits Context

Product Market Fit is only one of four required fits:

| Fit | Question |
|-----|---------|
| **Product Market Fit** | Does a meaningful segment desperately want this? |
| **Product Channel Fit** | Is the product built for how customers discover it? |
| **Channel Model Fit** | Does the business model work with the distribution channel economics? |
| **Model Market Fit** | Does the business model reach $100M+ at the market's preferred price/volume? |

**All four must be achieved simultaneously.** And in the AI era, all four can break simultaneously.
