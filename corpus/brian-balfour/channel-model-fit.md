## Definition

**Channel Model Fit:** Your business model economics must work with your chosen distribution channels.

This is the third of the Four Fits required to build a $100M business at venture speed.

---

## The ARPU ↔ CAC Spectrum

Every business lives on a spectrum:

- **Low ARPU** → must use low CAC channels (self-serve, viral, SEO, product-led)
- **High ARPU** → can use high CAC channels (enterprise sales, inside sales, paid acquisition)

**Channel Model Fit = your ARPU supports your required CAC while maintaining healthy unit economics.**

Classic examples of misfit:
- A freemium product with $10/month pricing **cannot** succeed through enterprise sales teams (CAC too high relative to LTV)
- A $50,000 annual software license **cannot** rely on viral social media growth (won't scale to volume needed)

---

## How AI Disrupts Channel Model Fit

### 1. Cost Increases Break GTM

Freemium products historically had marginal cost to serve free/freemium users. LLM costs change this:

- Traditional SaaS gross margins: typically above 70%
- **Lovable's gross margin: 35%** — and that doesn't include cost of serving free users

If you can't afford to serve free users, your PLG motion breaks. If you raise prices to compensate, you shift up the ARPU spectrum and may break channel fit.

### 2. Channel Collapse Triggers Monetization Changes

Products relying on SEO traffic face existential pressure as organic traffic decreases from AI answer engines.

The cascade:
- Less organic traffic → must shift to more expensive channels (paid advertising)
- More expensive channels → CAC increases → need higher ARPU to maintain unit economics
- Higher ARPU attempt → pricing change → churn risk

### 3. Shifts in User Adoption Behavior

AI can create instant shifts in user behavior:
- Users now expect near-zero friction trial (AI made this the norm)
- Higher-priced or complex products that still demand lengthy demos or large sign-up fees face headwind
- Tried-and-true channels (long content marketing funnels) may become far less effective overnight

---

## The Danger Zone

Companies in the Danger Zone face:

- **Stranded:** ARPU too low for human-powered sales, too high/complex for viral or paid channels
- **Massive churn** from competitor's AI-driven product (better product at same or lower price)
- **Ballooning CAC** and negative unit economics (as old channels degrade)
- **Forced overhauls:** pricing and product changes simultaneously under pressure
- **Increased competitive pressure** to move up or down market (but moving requires channel change)

---

## Channel Model Fit in the Product-Led Growth Context

**The PLG Channel Model Fit question:** Can you acquire, activate, and convert users at a price point where self-serve economics work?

Key variables:
- Product complexity (too complex = can't self-serve)
- ACV (too low = can't afford sales; too high = need sales)
- Margin (must be able to serve free tier profitably or nearly so)
- Time to value (must be fast enough for PLG conversion)

AI changes all four variables simultaneously for many products.

---

## Building Channel Model Fit in the AI Era

**Questions to answer:**
1. What does AI do to my cost to serve? (especially free/freemium tier)
2. What does AI do to my current acquisition channels? (especially SEO)
3. What does AI do to my competitors' pricing? (if they can offer it cheaper/free)
4. What CAC can my new-normal ARPU actually support?
5. Is there a channel that fits the new CAC ceiling?

**The sequence:** Don't change your channel before understanding your ARPU and margin. Don't change your pricing before understanding your channel options. All three must fit together.
