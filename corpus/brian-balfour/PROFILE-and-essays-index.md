## Core Intellectual Frameworks

### The Four Fits Framework
The central framework across his essay series. Four "fits" that must all align to reach $100M+ growth:

1. **Market-Product Fit** (not "Product-Market Fit" — the order matters)
   - The market exists first; you build a product to fit it
   - Key questions: Who are the exact customers? What are their exact pain points? What are your product's exact features?

2. **Product-Channel Fit**
   - Products are built to fit channels — channels are NOT built to fit products
   - Different product types have inherent channel affinities (e.g., viral features → viral channels; transactional → paid)
   - Key mistake: Trying to force a product into a channel it wasn't built for

3. **Channel-Model Fit**
   - Channels are determined by your monetization model
   - The ARPU-CAC spectrum: what you charge per user determines which channels are economically viable
   - Low ARPU (under ~$100/yr) → can't afford paid or sales; needs viral/content
   - High ARPU ($1K+/yr) → can support inside sales, paid ads
   - Key mistake: Trying to use paid acquisition for a product with too-low ARPU

4. **Model-Market Fit**
   - Your market size and customer profile influence your model
   - Reference: Christoph Janz's "Five Ways to Build a $100M Business" (flies, mice, rabbits, deer, elephants — sized by ARPU and customer count needed)

**The critical insight:** All four fits form an interconnected ecosystem. You cannot optimize one in isolation. This is why companies fail at moving up or down market — they change one fit without accounting for how it breaks the others.

### Growth Loops (vs. Funnels)

Funnels are a dead-end mental model. They're linear, have a defined end, and treat growth as a one-way input-output process.

**Growth loops** are closed systems where the output from one iteration feeds back in as input for the next. Each loop cycle compounds.

Examples of growth loops:
- **Viral loop:** User invites colleague → colleague signs up → colleague invites more colleagues
- **Content loop:** User creates content → content gets indexed → new user finds via SEO → signs up → creates content
- **Paid loop:** Revenue from customer → reinvested in paid acquisition → new customer → more revenue

Why loops beat funnels: Loops compound. Funnels don't.

### LinkedIn's AI-Assisted Growth Loop (2024)
Balfour and Fareed Mosavat analyzed LinkedIn's AI writing assistant loop:
- LinkedIn releases AI writing tool
- Users create content faster and in higher volume
- More content → more engagement → more reach → more people see LinkedIn's value → sign up → use AI tools to create content
- Result: 0 → 1M organic uniques/month in 3 months (one of the steepest content loop growth curves ever observed)

### North Star Metric Nuances
Key: Don't over-simplify to one metric. The "One Metric That Matters" (OMTM) philosophy is misleading because:
1. One metric can go up while the business gets weaker
2. Different parts of the funnel need different metrics
3. You want your team to understand the ecosystem, not just optimize a number
4. North star metrics change as the company grows

Better approach: North star → supporting metrics → leading indicators (a hierarchy, not a single number)
