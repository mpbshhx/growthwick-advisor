## Definition

**Model Market Fit:** Your chosen business model must align with how your target market prefers to buy and pay for solutions — AND that alignment must add up to $100M+ potential revenue.

This is the fourth and final of the Four Fits required to build a $100M business at venture speed.

---

## The Five Ways to Build a $100M Business

Framework from Christoph Janz (Point Nine Capital) — "Five Ways to Build a $100 Million Business":

| Type | Customers Needed | Price/Year | Examples |
|------|-----------------|------------|---------|
| Elephants | 1,000 | $100k+ | ServiceNow, Veeva |
| Moose | 10,000 | $10k+ | HubSpot, Zendesk |
| Rabbits | 100,000 | $1k | SurveyMonkey, Mailchimp, Gusto |
| Mice | 1M | $100 | Dropbox, Ipsy |
| Flies | 10M | $10 (ad-supported) | Facebook, Snapchat |

**Model Market Fit = Revenue Math Works:** If customers × ARPU ≥ $100M potential, you're above the diagonal line.

## How AI Disrupts Model Market Fit

### 1. Dramatic Reduction in ARPU

AI-driven tools often automate what was once high-value human labor. Competitors may drop the price 10x or offer it free.

If your business model assumed Rabbit-level pricing ($1k/year) but AI enables a Mouse-level product ($100/year), your math breaks — you now need 10x the customers for the same revenue target.

### 2. TAM Contraction or Fragmentation

- **TAM contraction:** Customer support teams shrank as AI replaced work. Seat-based vendors suddenly have fewer seats to sell.
- **TAM fragmentation:** Even if market is still there and ARPU is same, AI creates new winners with network effects, making it harder for incumbents to keep market share.

### 3. SAM Contraction

Even when the total addressable market exists:
- AI creates new winners who capture network effects
- Incumbents find they can only address a fraction of their former serviceable market

### 4. New Buyer Personas with Different Economics

The traditional IT buyer focused on features and integrations. The AI buyer often comes from:
- Operations (evaluating against headcount savings)
- Finance (evaluating ROI on process automation)
- Business units (evaluating outcomes, not features)

This creates completely different:
- Approval processes
- Budget sources (not IT budget, often operations/headcount budget)
- Implementation timelines
- Pricing expectations (outcome-based vs. seat-based)

## Case Study: Intercom Fin

**The Model Market Fit problem:** Intercom was a seat-based customer support tool. As AI replaced human agents, seat count would shrink → TAM contraction.

**The solution:** Launched Fin, AI-powered customer support at 99 cents per resolved ticket.

**The math:** At 99 cents/ticket, needs 100M+ tickets/year to reach Model Market Fit. As of August 2025, Fin resolves more than 1 million tickets per week (~50M+/year and growing).

**What this required:** Complete business model transformation, not just a product feature. New pricing model (usage-based vs. seat-based), new buyer (head of CX vs. IT buyer), new value proposition (tickets resolved vs. seats managed).

---

## Case Study: Replit

**The Model Market Fit challenge:** Replit has 40M users, 175k paying customers (~0.44% conversion rate). Revenue grew 50x in 12 months.

**The lesson:** Good product + big market + massive interest ≠ guaranteed Model Market Fit.

The 0.44% conversion rate raises questions: Is the 99.56% who don't pay a monetization problem (wrong model) or a product problem (not enough value for paid)? At scale, even 0.44% can work — but the math must reach $100M.

## Rabbits Becoming Moose (and Vice Versa)

AI is reshuffling which tier a product belongs to:

**Rabbits becoming Moose:** Products that once needed 100,000 customers at $1k/year can now deliver $10k/year of value through AI automation (doing work that previously required 10 people). Examples: AI-native accounting tools, AI-native legal research tools.

**Mice turning into Flies:** Products that charged $100/year find AI commoditizes them, forcing ad-supported models to stay competitive.

**The danger:** Getting stuck between tiers. A product that used to be Rabbit-sized ($1k) but faces Fly-level competition ($10 or free) with Moose-level CAC requirements.

## Building Model Market Fit in the AI Era

**Key questions:**
1. What does AI do to willingness to pay in my market?
2. What does AI do to the number of buyers/seats in my market?
3. What pricing model does my new buyer persona prefer? (seat, usage, outcome)
4. Does my new pricing model × addressable market still reach $100M?
5. What would it take to shift up one tier (Rabbit → Moose) using AI to deliver more value?

**The diagnostic:** Build the revenue math bottom-up. New buyer persona × new ARPU × new market size = does it still work?
