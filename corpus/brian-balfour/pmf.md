# Why Product Market Fit Isn't Enough — Brian Balfour

## The Core Observation

After building, advising, or investing in 40+ tech companies:
- **Smooth Sailers**: Growth comes easily despite organizational chaos, not executing "best" growth practices, and missing low-hanging fruit
- **Tugboats**: Despite best practices, great teams, and picking low-hanging fruit, they struggle

The difference is NOT any of the common mantras.

## It's Not Just Great Product

"Build a great product" is the go-to answer, but:
- There are great products that never reach $100M+
- There are terrible products (e.g., Workday at $20B) that reach far greater

"Build a great product" leads to the **Product Death Cycle**: "If you build it, they will come" → they don't → add more features → still don't come.

## It's Not Just Product Market Fit

While product market fit is a component, we've taken it to the extreme with tunnel vision. "Product Market fit is the only thing that matters" — it is NOT the only thing that matters.

**Example**: Soldsie (tool for selling on Instagram/Facebook) had all product market fit signals:
- Solid NPS
- Good retention  
- Organic growth

Yet growth was slow and not at venture-backed pace. They couldn't reach $100M+.

## It's Definitely Not Growth Hacking

The term "growth hacking" morphed into **hacktics** — tips, tricks, secrets that might unlock growth. Problems:
- Short-lived
- Never sustainable

Before tactics → growth process. Before growth process → strategy.

## What IS the Difference?

The $100M+ companies make **four pieces of a puzzle fit together**: The Four Fits Framework.

1. Market Product Fit
2. Product Channel Fit  
3. Channel Model Fit
4. Model Market Fit

These four fits must align together as an ecosystem — not in isolation.
