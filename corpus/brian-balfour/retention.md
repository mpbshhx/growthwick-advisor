# Retention Is King — Brian Balfour

## Core Framework: Flat Retention Curves = Market Product Fit

Brian Balfour's key contribution to retention thinking: **flat retention curves are the primary quantitative indicator of Market Product Fit.**

### The Retention Curve Test

If your retention curves flatten (users who stay past a certain point continue to stay), you have Market Product Fit. If curves continuously decline to zero, you don't.

**Exceptions to flat curves:**
- Online dating: Natural churn is baked into success (finding a partner = leaving)
- Insurance tech: Success = finding a plan = natural churn

For 80%+ of B2C and B2B products, flat retention curves are the target.

### What Flat Retention Curves Mean

Flat retention + direct traffic growth = the product grows naturally without additional marketing effort.

The test question: *"If you turned off all your marketing efforts today, would you keep growing?"*
- Answer should be: **Yes** (maybe slowly, but still growing)

## Retention as a Signal Across the Four Fits

### Market Product Fit: Is the product solving a real problem?
- Flat retention curves = yes
- Declining curves = no (product isn't delivering enough value)

### Product Channel Fit: Which channels retain users best?
- Users from viral channels often have different retention than paid users
- Retention by acquisition source reveals Product Channel Fit alignment

### Channel Model Fit: Does revenue per retained user justify channel cost?
- ARPU × retention duration must exceed CAC for the math to work
- High retention extends LTV, expanding which channels become viable

### Model Market Fit: Is the retained market large enough?
- Strong retention in a tiny niche ≠ $100M company
- Need: large enough market to reach scale

## The Retention Loop

Retention is not just a metric — it's a system:
1. Users who stay → word of mouth → organic acquisition
2. Organic acquisition → lower CAC → more budget for growth
3. More users → more data → better product → better retention

This is why retention is foundational to all growth loops.

## Key Retention Principles from Balfour

1. **Measure retention by cohort** — aggregate retention hides decay in recent cohorts
2. **Segment by acquisition channel** — different channels bring users with different retention profiles
3. **Retention is a product problem first** — marketing can't fix a retention hole
4. **Look for the "aha moment"** — users who reach it have dramatically higher retention
5. **North Star Metric must correlate with retention** — beware metrics that look good but don't predict retention

## North Star Metric Warning

From "Don't Let Your North Star Metric Deceive You": Blindly following one metric is dangerous. Casey Winters (Pinterest): "The search for one key metric over-simplifies how the ecosystem works."

Real retention thinking requires understanding multiple interdependent signals, not optimizing one number.
