# How Claude Is Building A Unique Growth Engine Out of User-Created Apps

By Brian Balfour and Fareed Mosavat | July 21, 2025

---

## Overview

One of the things Brian has been watching closely: how AI is creating new types of Growth Loops that haven't existed before.

Two years ago he wrote about LinkedIn's AI-enhanced content loop that generated 1M uniques per month in record time.

Now, Anthropic launched a new Claude feature called "AI-powered apps" — interactive AI-powered apps built, hosted, and shared directly in the Claude app. Essentially vibe-coding within Claude rather than using purpose-built tools like Cursor, Bolt, or Lovable.

---

## What Are Claude AI-Powered Apps?

The ability to build, host, and share interactive AI-powered apps directly in the Claude app.

Examples:
- **Educational Tools:** A high school teacher created an SAT prep app generating personalized practice questions based on student weak areas. Students log in with their Claude account; app adapts difficulty in real-time.
- **Business Utilities:** A product manager built a requirements analyzer that reads uploaded PRDs and generates test cases, edge scenarios, and risks. Teams across her company use it daily.
- **Data Analysis:** A sales ops team created an app that ingests CSV exports and lets anyone ask in plain English: "Which territories are underperforming this quarter?"

Fareed Mosavat built an app for his kid's SAT studying: "Kind of a pain in the butt in something like Lovable, right? Because I got to get an OpenAI API key... I need to create accounts and all this sort of stuff. I'm not planning on charging for it."

---

## What Makes Claude's Approach Unique

When someone uses your AI-powered app built on Claude:

1. **They authenticate with their existing Claude account** — No separate login system for you to build or maintain.

2. **Their API usage counts against their subscription, not yours** — No worrying about managing model costs. If your app goes viral and gets a million users, you don't get a million-dollar API bill. Each user's prompts count against their own Claude subscription (Free, Pro, or Max).

3. **No one manages API keys** — Forget about users needing to enter their API key, usage limits, or security concerns. Claude handles all of that.

These constraints create a unique growth model for Anthropic and Claude.

---

## The Core Growth Loop

Claude's AI-powered apps create an **AI-powered content loop** — a User Generated, User Distributed Content Loop (similar to Eventbrite's model).

### The Loop:

**Step 1:** New Claude user signs up or returns to Claude

**Step 2:** User creates an AI-powered app (no code required)
- Solves a specific problem by building a lightweight application directly within Claude
- No technical skills required — just natural language instructions
- Examples: custom SAT tutor, project tracker, specialized calculator

**Step 3:** Creator shares app with their network
- Internal sharing within a company
- Posting in professional communities
- Sending a link to friends and family
- Distribution is organic and targeted

**Step 4 (back to Step 1):** Recipients must authenticate with Claude to use the app
- This drives new or returning Claude users
- The app won't work without a Claude account

**Step 5:** Some percentage of new users become creators themselves
- Once in the Claude ecosystem, they discover they can build their own AI-powered apps
- The cycle keeps repeating

---

## Nested Loops: The Real Power

Each app built with Claude has its own set of growth loops driving users and usage (and therefore Claude).

- A project management tool built on Claude might have sharing and collaboration features driving additional invitations
- A specialized calculator might get passed around industry forums
- Each app becomes a distribution channel for Claude itself

This creates "nested loops" — the core creation-sharing loop at the platform level, plus individual sharing and engagement loops within each application.

---

## What Creators Get vs. Give Up

### What creators get:
- **Free hosting and infrastructure** — No AWS accounts, no deployment pipelines, no server management
- **No API costs or key management** — When someone uses your app, their Claude subscription covers costs
- **Built-in authentication system** — User management is Claude's problem. No password reset flows, no GDPR headaches
- **Future distribution potential** — Gallery discovery mechanism signaled

### What creators give up:
- **User ownership/data** — No full control over email lists, usage analytics, or direct user communication outside Claude's ecosystem
- **Platform independence** — App is tied to Claude's infrastructure; can't easily migrate
- **Monetization options (currently)** — Must code in your own payment system; no native monetization via Claude

---

## The Constraint Creates the Opportunity

Most content loops fail at the creation step — converting enough users to actual creators requires overcoming significant friction: technical barriers, cost concerns, time investment, unclear value exchange.

Claude has designed constraints that encourage creation for a different part of the market:

**The spectrum of app builders:**
- **Full control builders** → Cursor, Claude Code
- **Less technical builders willing to give up some control** → Bolt, Lovable
- **Even less technical, longer tail** → Claude AI-powered apps (Claude's target)

Claude creates even more constraints, enabling an even longer tail of application building for less technical people.

---

## Key Uncertainties

Brian is clear he doesn't know if this will work because:

1. AI app development is crowded with purpose-built products for the exact use case
2. AI app creation within Claude will need to compete for attention of all the other things someone can do with Claude
3. Claude's artifacts approach feels like the opposite end of the strategic spectrum from their other initiatives focusing on hardcore developers and enterprise customers

---

## Key Insights for Product and Growth Leaders

### AI is enabling new variations on growth loops
New technology capabilities enable new growth mechanics. When non-technical creation becomes possible, the growth opportunities shift too.

### Sometimes the constraint is the opportunity
Most platforms view constraints as problems to solve. Claude flipped this thinking — instead of competing with full-featured development platforms, they embraced severe limitations and used those constraints to enable a different value exchange.

### Always align incentives across all participants in the loop
Claude's model works (if it works) because every participant gets immediate value:
- Creators solve their own problems first
- End users get utility without setup friction
- Claude captures user growth and engagement

### Start constrained, expand deliberately
Fareed: "I like what they've done with making this very, very constrained... I like that it's pretty small and narrow. I like that it really encourages tinkering and ease of use."

The temptation with new growth mechanics is to immediately build for every use case. Starting small creates room to learn before the stakes get higher.
